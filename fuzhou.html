Key Fact

Name Origin

Fuzhou (福州) means 'Prosperous City' or 'Blessed City.' It has been an important port and cultural center in Fujian for over 2,200 years, serving as the capital of the Minyue kingdom and later as a key maritime city during the Tang and Song dynasties.

Education & Universities

Fuzhou is a respected education center in southeast China. Fuzhou University is a key national university known for engineering and chemistry. Fujian Normal University is one of the oldest normal universities in China, and Fujian Medical University is also well-regarded.

Metro & Transportation

Fuzhou Metro has 5 lines connecting the city center, railway stations, and the airport. It is modern and efficient, but reaching Drum Mountain, the Min River area, and ancient towns is easier with a private car.

Natural Resources & Environment

Fuzhou lies on the Min River near the coast of the Taiwan Strait, surrounded by low hills and lush greenery. The city is known for its many Banyan trees, mild climate, and frequent rainfall. It has a relaxed, green atmosphere compared to busier coastal cities.

Getting to Fuzhou: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Fuzhou and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Fuzhou Changle International Airport (FOC)

Southeast coast · 45 km to downtown

The main airport serving Fuzhou and northern Fujian, with domestic and international flights. A private car takes 50–70 minutes to downtown hotels.

Nearby Airports

Xiamen Gaoqi, Quanzhou Jinjiang, Ningde Xiapu

Regional airports serve nearby Fujian cities. FOC is the main airport for Fuzhou itself and northern Fujian.

🚄 Railway Stations

Fuzhou Railway Station

Main city center station · Gulou district

The main railway station serving conventional and high-speed trains to Xiamen, Quanzhou, Shanghai, and other cities. Connected to Metro Line 1.

Fuzhou South Railway Station

High-speed rail hub · Cangshan district

The main high-speed rail station south of the city, serving bullet trains to Xiamen, Shenzhen, Shanghai, and Hangzhou.

Fuzhou West Railway Station

New western hub · West Fuzhou

A newer station serving high-speed and suburban trains to the western districts and inland Fujian.

Fuzhou East Railway Station

Suburban east station

Serves selected conventional and regional trains, mainly for suburban travel east of the city center.

Minhou Railway Station

Suburban HSR · Minhou county

A smaller high-speed station serving the Minhou area west of Fuzhou. Useful for selected regional routes.

Food & Shopping in Fuzhou

🍽️ Must-Try Fuzhou Cuisine

Fuzhou Fish Balls (Yu Wan)

Springy fish balls with a savory meat filling. A signature Fuzhou snack found in restaurants and street stalls.

Buddha Jumps Over the Wall (Fo Tiao Qiang)

A luxurious soup with abalone, sea cucumber, shark fin, and chicken, slow-cooked in a clay pot. Fuzhou's most famous banquet dish.

Oyster Omelette (Hao Jian)

Fresh oysters fried with eggs and sweet potato starch, served with chili sauce. A popular Fujian coastal snack.

Rice Paste and Rice Cakes

Soft rice paste noodles and sweet rice cakes are common Fuzhou breakfast and snack items.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Fuzhou

Meet the traditional arts that have shaped Fuzhou's identity for centuries.

🎭 Min Opera (Fuzhou Opera)

A traditional opera form from Fujian, known for its melodious singing, elegant movements, and elaborate costumes. It is one of the major opera styles of southeast China.

Where to find it: Fuzhou theatres and cultural festivals.

🎨 Fuzhou Bodiless Lacquerware

A unique craft where objects are made from lacquer-soaked cloth and then polished and decorated. It is one of China's three great lacquer arts.

Where to find it: Lacquerware shops and museums in Fuzhou.

🎋 Fuzhou Soft Wood Carving

Traditional wood carving using soft, lightweight wood to create intricate figures and scenes.

Where to find it: Craft markets and cultural centers in Fuzhou.

🎶 Fujian Nanyin Music

An ancient genre of Chinese chamber music, recognized by UNESCO, featuring flutes, lutes, and percussion.

Where to find it: Cultural performances and traditional tea houses in Fuzhou.

✂️ Fuzhou Paper Cutting

Delicate folk paper art with themes from Fujian opera, local legends, and auspicious symbols.

Where to find it: Folk art shops and festival markets.

🍵 Fujian Tea Ceremony Arts

The traditional arts of tea preparation, tasting, and appreciation, deeply tied to Fujian's oolong and jasmine tea cultures.

Where to find it: Tea houses and specialty tea shops across Fuzhou.

Fuzhou Local Secrets

Insider tips to help you experience Fuzhou like a local.

🍵

Try Tea in a Traditional House

Fuzhou has many tea houses in the Three Lanes and Seven Alleys area. Order oolong tea and take a slow break from sightseeing.

🌳

Look Up at the Banyan Trees

Fuzhou is called the City of Banyans. The ancient trees shading the streets are as much a sight as the buildings.

🚗

Drum Mountain is Best by Car

The mountain roads and temple area are spread out. A private car can drop you at the best viewpoints and wait.

🌙

Walk the Old Lanes at Night

Three Lanes and Seven Alleys are quieter and more atmospheric in the evening after the day-trip crowds leave.

🍜

Eat Fish Balls for Breakfast

Locals eat fish balls and rice paste for breakfast. It's a delicious, light start to the day.

🛍️

Buy Tea Direct from Shops

Fujian tea shops offer tastings and better prices than airport souvenir stores. Ask for local oolong or jasmine.

Best Time to Visit Fuzhou

Plan your trip around Fuzhou's seasons for the best experience.

🌸

Spring (Mar–May)

15–25°C · Mild & Rainy

Warm and green, with flowers and fresh tea harvests. Rain is common, so bring an umbrella.

☀️

Summer (Jun–Aug)

26–35°C · Hot & Humid

Hot and humid, with occasional typhoons. Early mornings and evenings are best for outdoor activities.

🍂

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

18–28°C · Warm & Clear

The best time to visit. Comfortable temperatures, less rain, and clear skies for mountain views.

❄️

Winter (Dec–Feb)

8–15°C · Cool & Damp

Mild by northern standards, but damp and gray. Fewer tourists and good prices on hotels.
