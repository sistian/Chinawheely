Name Origin

The name "Chongqing" (重庆) means "Double Celebration." In 1189, the Southern Song Emperor Guangzong was promoted to prince here and later ascended the throne — a "double celebration" that gave the city its name. During WWII, it served as China's wartime capital. Today it's one of China's four direct-controlled municipalities and the gateway to the Three Gorges.

Education & Universities

Chongqing is a major educational hub in western China. Chongqing University (founded 1929) is renowned for engineering and architecture. Southwest University is one of China's largest by enrollment. The city also hosts Third Military Medical University and Chongqing University of Posts and Telecommunications.

Metro & Transportation

Chongqing's metro system is a marvel of engineering, with 12 lines and 500+ km of track winding through mountains, crossing rivers, and even passing through buildings. The famous Liziba Station has a metro line passing directly through a residential building. However, the mountainous terrain makes walking challenging — a private car is often the most comfortable way to navigate.

Natural Resources & Environment

Chongqing sits at the confluence of the Yangtze River and the Jialing River, surrounded by mountains on three sides. The city is famous for its foggy weather, earning it the nickname "Fog City." The surrounding area includes the Wulong Karst (UNESCO World Heritage) and the dramatic Three Gorges region.

Top Chongqing Attractions

Discover the Mountain City's most spectacular sights with a private driver.

🏮

Hongya Cave (Hongya Dong)

Stilt Houses • Night Views

A stunning complex of traditional stilt houses built into the riverside cliff, illuminated at night to resemble the bathhouse from Spirited Away. The 11-story structure offers restaurants, shops, and breathtaking views of the Jialing River.

🚠

Yangtze River Cableway

Scenic Crossing • City Views

One of China's oldest and most scenic cableways, crossing the Yangtze River with panoramic views of the city's dramatic skyline. The 4-minute ride connects Yuzhong District to Nan'an District.

🌃

Chongqing Night View

Nanshan • One Tree Pavilion

Chongqing's night view is considered one of the top three in China. The best viewpoint is Nanshan One Tree Pavilion, overlooking the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers.

🗿

Dazu Rock Carvings

UNESCO World Heritage • Buddhist Art

A series of 7th–13th century Buddhist, Confucian, and Taoist rock carvings featuring over 50,000 statues. The most famous site is Baodingshan, with intricate depictions of Buddhist hell and paradise.

🎭

Ciqikou Ancient Town

Ming & Qing Dynasty • Porcelain Port

A well-preserved ancient town on the Jialing River that was once a major porcelain production center. Today, it's a charming maze of narrow alleys, traditional teahouses, street food vendors, and craft shops.

🌊

Three Gorges Museum

Yangtze History • Dam Exhibition

A comprehensive museum documenting the history, culture, and ecology of the Yangtze River and the Three Gorges region. The exhibits include artifacts rescued before the dam flooded ancient sites. Free admission.

Getting to Chongqing: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Chongqing and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Chongqing Jiangbei International Airport (CKG)

Northeast of the city · 25 km to Jiefangbei

CKG is one of China's top 10 busiest airports and the main gateway to western China. It has three terminals: Terminal 1 (closed for renovation), Terminal 2 (domestic flights), and Terminal 3 (international and major domestic flights, opened in 2017). The airport metro line connects to the city center, but Chongqing's mountainous terrain makes navigating with luggage extremely difficult. A private car is the most comfortable option, taking 40–60 minutes to downtown.

Nearby Airports

Wanzhou Wuqiao Airport · Qianjiang Wulingshan Airport

Chongqing's vast municipality includes several smaller regional airports. Wanzhou Wuqiao Airport (WXN) serves the Three Gorges area, while Qianjiang Wulingshan Airport (JIQ) serves the southeastern region. For most international travelers, CKG is the only relevant airport.

🚄 Railway Stations

Chongqing North Station

High-speed hub · 20 min to Jiefangbei

The main station for high-speed trains to Chengdu (1.5 hours), Wuhan (6 hours), and Guiyang (2 hours). It has two squares: North Square (HSR) and South Square (conventional). A private car pickup is essential as the station is large and taxi queues are long.

Chongqing West Station

Newest station · Western trains

Opened in 2018, this is one of China's largest railway stations. It serves high-speed trains to western and southern China, including Kunming and Guangzhou. Located in the Shapingba district, it's convenient for western Chongqing hotels.

Chongqing Station (Caiyuanba)

Historic station · Near Yangtze River

Chongqing's oldest station, located near the Yangtze River in Yuzhong District. It mainly serves conventional trains to nearby destinations. Its central location makes it convenient for hotels in the Jiefangbei area.

Shapingba Station

Suburban HSR · University district

Serves high-speed trains on the Chengdu-Chongqing line. Convenient for travelers staying in the Shapingba university district or visiting the nearby Ciqikou Ancient Town.

Wanzhou North Station

Three Gorges area · 2.5 hours from downtown

Serves the Wanzhou district in the northeast of the municipality, gateway to the Three Gorges. Useful for travelers heading directly to the dam area without passing through downtown Chongqing.

Food & Shopping in Chongqing

🍽️ Must-Try Chongqing Cuisine

Chongqing Hot Pot

The city's most famous dish. A bubbling cauldron of spicy chili oil and Sichuan peppercorns, where you cook thinly sliced meat, vegetables, and tofu at the table. The numbing-spicy (mala) flavor is addictive. Ask your driver for a local favorite away from tourist traps.

Spicy Chicken (La Zi Ji)

Bite-sized chicken pieces deep-fried with a mountain of dried red chilies and Sichuan peppercorns. The challenge is finding the chicken among the chilies — and the flavor is explosive. A Chongqing classic best enjoyed with cold beer.

Chongqing Noodles (Xiao Mian)

The city's signature breakfast — wheat noodles in a spicy, savory broth with minced pork, peanuts, and scallions. Every street corner has a xiao mian stall. Your driver knows the best ones with the most authentic flavor.

Stuffed Tofu (Rongchang Dou Fu)

Soft tofu stuffed with seasoned pork and vegetables, then braised in a savory sauce. A specialty from Rongchang District that showcases the region's skill with tofu. Milder than hot pot but equally delicious.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Jiefangbei Pedestrian Street

Chongqing's most famous shopping and commercial center, surrounded by skyscrapers and luxury brands. The nearby Bayi Road Food Street is perfect for trying local snacks. Your driver can drop you at the pedestrian entrance and arrange a pickup time.

Ciqikou Ancient Town

A historic porcelain port turned tourist destination, filled with craft shops, teahouses, and street food vendors. Great for picking up traditional Chongqing crafts and souvenirs. The narrow alleys are best explored on foot.

Three Gorges Plaza

A modern shopping complex in the Shapingba district with international brands, restaurants, and entertainment. Convenient for travelers staying in the western part of the city. Your driver can navigate the busy streets and drop you at the main entrance.

Guanyinqiao Shopping District

One of Chongqing's busiest commercial areas, with shopping malls, electronics markets, and trendy restaurants. The pedestrian street is especially lively in the evening. Your driver can pick you up after shopping and take you directly to your hotel.

🎁 What to Buy in Chongqing

Chongqing Hot Pot Base

Bring the taste of Chongqing home with authentic hot pot soup base packets. Available at supermarkets and specialty shops. Look for brands like Qinma or Dezhuang for the most authentic flavor.

Rongchang Pottery

A tradition dating back over 800 years, Rongchang pottery is known for its fine clay, smooth texture, and natural color. Master potters still use traditional techniques to create teapots, bowls, and decorative items. The craft is listed as national intangible cultural heritage.

Shu Embroidery

One of China's four great embroidery styles, Shu embroidery is known for its fine needlework, realistic depictions, and vibrant colors. The technique requires years of training and produces works that are almost indistinguishable from paintings. Traditional motifs include pandas, fish, and landscapes.

Three Gorges Ink Stones

Hand-carved ink stones from the Three Gorges region, used in traditional Chinese calligraphy. Each stone has unique natural patterns. A meaningful gift for art lovers. Available at the Three Gorges Museum and select craft shops.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Chongqing

Meet the traditional arts that have defined Chongqing for centuries — and the artisans keeping them alive.

🎭 Sichuan Opera (Chuanju)

UNESCO-listed intangible heritage. Sichuan Opera is famous for its face-changing (bianlian) technique, where performers magically change masks in the blink of an eye. The art combines singing, acrobatics, comedy, and puppetry. The fire-spitting and rolling-light acts are breathtaking.

Where to experience it: Hongyadong Theater, Ciqikou Theater, or Shufeng Yayun Teahouse. Ask your driver to book tickets in advance.

🏺 Rongchang Pottery

A tradition dating back over 800 years, Rongchang pottery is known for its fine clay, smooth texture, and natural color. Master potters still use traditional techniques to create teapots, bowls, and decorative items. The craft is listed as national intangible cultural heritage.

Where to find it: Rongchang District (2 hours from downtown), Ciqikou Ancient Town, or the Three Gorges Museum gift shop.

🎨 Shu Embroidery

One of China's four great embroidery styles, Shu embroidery is known for its fine needlework, realistic depictions, and vibrant colors. The technique requires years of training and produces works that are almost indistinguishable from paintings. Traditional motifs include pandas, fish, and landscapes.

Where to find it: Ciqikou Ancient Town, Chongqing Arts and Crafts Museum, or high-end craft shops in Jiefangbei.

🎵 Sichuan Qingyin (Clear Tone)

A traditional form of Sichuan folk singing accompanied by traditional instruments like the pipa and erhu. The songs tell stories of daily life, love, and local legends. The art form has been passed down through generations of performers and is a living part of Chongqing's cultural heritage.

Where to experience it: Traditional teahouses in Ciqikou or cultural performances at the Chongqing Grand Theatre.

🪁 Chongqing Kite Making

Traditional Chongqing kites are framed with bamboo and painted with birds, butterflies, or characters from folklore. The Shayan kite tradition, passed down through generations of craftsmen, is known for its perfect balance and elegant designs.

Where to find it: Look for kite craftsmen at the Temple Fair during Spring Festival, or visit traditional craft shops near Shichahai.

🎭 Dazu Rock Carving Craftsmanship

The stone carving techniques used to create the Dazu Rock Carvings have been passed down through generations of master craftsmen. These artisans combine Buddhist iconography with local folk art, creating works that are both spiritually significant and aesthetically stunning.

Where to find it: Dazu Rock Carvings site (2 hours from Chongqing), where you can see artisans at work and purchase small carved souvenirs.

Chongqing Local Secrets

Insider tips to help you experience Chongqing like a local.

🌶️

Start with Mild Hot Pot

If you're not used to spicy food, ask for "wei la" (mildly spicy) or "yuan yang" (half spicy, half clear broth). The numbing sensation from Sichuan peppercorns can be overwhelming for first-timers. Your driver can recommend beginner-friendly hot pot restaurants.

🚇

Use the Metro for Short Trips

Chongqing's metro is efficient and affordable, but never rely on it during rush hour (7:30–9:30 AM, 5:30–7:30 PM). For short trips within the city, the metro is great. For anything with luggage or during peak times, a private car is essential.

🌃

See the Night View from Nanshan

The Nanshan One Tree Pavilion offers the best night view of Chongqing, but it's crowded on weekends. Go on a weekday evening, arrive before sunset, and bring a jacket — it gets windy at the top. Your driver can take you up and wait while you enjoy the view.

🍜

Eat Xiao Mian for Breakfast

Xiao mian (small noodles) is Chongqing's signature breakfast. Look for stalls with long lines of locals — that's where the best flavor is. Ask for "gan ban" (dry mix) if you prefer less soup. It's spicy, savory, and the perfect start to a day of exploring.

🏮

Visit Hongya Cave Early

Hongya Cave is magical at night but extremely crowded after 7 PM. Visit around 5 PM to explore the shops and restaurants, then stay for the lights to turn on at dusk. The best photo spot is from across the river on Qiansimen Bridge.

🚗

Book a Private Car for Mountain Roads

Chongqing's roads are a maze of elevated highways, steep hills, and sharp turns. GPS often fails here because the city is built in 3D — roads stack on top of each other. A local driver who knows the terrain is invaluable, especially for trips to Dazu or Wulong.

Best Time to Visit Chongqing

Plan your trip around Chongqing's seasons for the best experience.

🌸

Spring (Mar–May)

15–25°C · Mild & Humid

Pleasant temperatures and blooming flowers. The fog is less dense, and the city is green and vibrant. Ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the mountain scenery.

☀️

Summer (Jun–Aug)

25–40°C · Hot & Humid

Extremely hot and humid — Chongqing is known as one of China's "Three Furnaces." Outdoor activities are challenging. Best for indoor attractions, hot pot (surprisingly popular in summer), and evening river cruises.

🍂

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

15–28°C · Cool & Clear

The best season to visit Chongqing. Clear skies, comfortable temperatures, and less fog. Perfect for night views, outdoor dining, and day trips to Dazu or Wulong. The autumn foliage in the mountains is spectacular.

❄️

Winter (Dec–Feb)

5–12°C · Cold & Foggy

Cold and damp with frequent fog. Hot pot season is in full swing, and the city feels cozy. Indoor attractions like museums and teahouses are ideal. The fog can limit visibility for night views, but the atmosphere is mystical.

Useful Chinese Phrases for Chongqing

A few key phrases to help you navigate Chongqing.

你好 (Nǐ hǎo)

Hello / Hi

The most basic greeting. Use it when meeting your driver, entering shops, or starting conversations.

谢谢 (Xièxiè)

Thank you

Essential politeness. Use it after receiving help, service, or directions.

多少钱 (Duōshao qián?)

How much is this?

Useful for shopping, street food, and markets. Bargaining is common in tourist areas.

我要去 (Wǒ yào qù...)

I want to go to...

Tell your driver where you want to go. For example: "Wǒ yào qù Hóngyá Dòng" (I want to go to Hongya Cave).

不要辣 (Bú yào là)

Not spicy / No chili

Crucial in Chongqing! Most dishes are spicy by default. Use this phrase when ordering food if you can't handle the heat.

厕所在哪里 (Cèsuǒ zài nǎlǐ?)

Where is the bathroom?

Essential for navigating Chongqing's maze of streets. Public restrooms are available but can be hard to find.
