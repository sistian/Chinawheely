Key Fact

Name Origin

Jinan (济南) means 'South of the Ji River,' referring to the ancient Ji River that once flowed north of the city. It has been an important cultural and administrative center in Shandong for over 4,000 years, with evidence of Longshan culture settlements in the area.

Education & Universities

Jinan is an important education center in Shandong. Shandong University is one of China's most prestigious universities, with a beautiful campus and strong programs in medicine, literature, and engineering. Shandong Normal University and Shandong University of Finance and Economics are also well-regarded.

Metro & Transportation

Jinan Metro has 3 lines connecting the city center, eastern high-tech zone, and some suburban areas. The city is also famous for its flat, bike-friendly layout. A private car is ideal for visiting springs, Daming Lake, and day trips to Mount Tai.

Natural Resources & Environment

Jinan is renowned as the City of Springs, with over 70 natural springs including Baotu Spring, Black Tiger Spring, and Five Dragon Pool. Daming Lake and Thousand Buddha Mountain add greenery to the urban core. The city enjoys a warm temperate climate with hot, rainy summers.

Top Jinan Attractions

Discover Jinan's springs, lakes, and temples with a private English-speaking driver.

💧

Baotu Spring

Famous Spring • City Symbol

The most famous of Jinan's 72 named springs, Baotu Spring has been bubbling up for over 3,500 years. The surrounding park is one of the city's most beloved spots.

🏞️

Daming Lake

Historic Lake • Gardens

A large, scenic lake in the city center surrounded by pavilions, gardens, and willow trees. Boating on the lake is a classic Jinan experience.

⛰️

Thousand Buddha Mountain

Buddhist Mountain • City Views

A forested mountain on the southern edge of the city with ancient Buddha statues, temples, and panoramic views over Jinan.

🐅

Black Tiger Spring

Natural Spring • Local Life

A powerful spring where locals gather daily to collect fresh drinking water. It's a great place to see everyday Jinan life.

🏛️

Shandong Provincial Museum

History • Artifacts

One of the largest provincial museums in China, with extensive collections of Shandong's ancient history, calligraphy, and art.

🛍️

Furong Street

Food Street • Nightlife

A narrow historic lane packed with street food vendors, snack stalls, and small shops. The best place to try local Jinan snacks in the evening.

Getting to Jinan: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Jinan and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Jinan Yaoqiang International Airport (TNA)

Northeast of the city · 30 km to downtown

The main airport serving Jinan and Shandong province, with domestic and some international flights. A private car takes 40–60 minutes to downtown hotels.

Nearby Airports

Qingdao Jiaodong, Weifang Nanyin, Tai'an

Travelers heading to Mount Tai sometimes fly via Jinan or use high-speed rail from Qingdao. TNA is the main airport for Jinan itself.

🚄 Railway Stations

Jinan West Railway Station

Main HSR hub · Western district

The primary high-speed rail station for Jinan, serving trains to Beijing, Shanghai, Qingdao, and Tai'an. Connected to the city by bus and taxi.

Jinan Railway Station

Historic city center · Tianqiao district

The older main station near the city center, serving conventional and some high-speed trains. Convenient for hotels near Daming Lake and Baotu Spring.

Jinan East Railway Station

Eastern suburb · Licheng district

A newer station serving high-speed trains on the Jinan-Qingdao and other eastern routes. Useful for travelers heading to the eastern high-tech zone.

Daminghu Railway Station

Near Daming Lake · Central Jinan

A smaller station serving selected conventional trains, convenient for the Daming Lake and old city area.

Zhangqiu Railway Station

Suburban station · Zhangqiu district

Serves conventional and high-speed trains on the Jinan-Qingdao line. Useful for travelers staying in the eastern suburbs.

Food & Shopping in Jinan

🍽️ Must-Try Jinan Cuisine

Sweet and Sour Carp (Tang Cu Li Yu)

Jinan's most famous dish, featuring crispy yellow river carp in a sweet and sour sauce. A must-try at local restaurants.

Jinan Youxuan (Fried Twisted Dough)

A crispy, savory fried dough twist, a popular street snack found at markets and food stalls.

Yellow River Carp Noodles

Hand-pulled noodles with carp slices in a rich, savory broth. A hearty local specialty.

Jinan Pancakes (Youxuan Bing)

Thin, crispy savory pancakes often eaten for breakfast or as a snack.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Jinan

Meet the traditional arts that have shaped Jinan's identity for centuries.

🎭 Shandong Opera (Lv Opera)

One of China's major regional opera forms, known for its expressive singing, vivid stories, and strong local dialect. It is especially popular in rural Shandong.

Where to find it: Local theaters and cultural festivals in Jinan.

🎨 Jinan Shadow Puppetry

Traditional leather shadow puppet performances using backlit screens, telling stories from history and folklore.

Where to find it: Folk art centers and temple fairs in Jinan.

🖌️ Qilu Seal Carving

The ancient art of carving personal and official seals from stone, a refined tradition in Shandong culture.

Where to find it: Art supply shops and calligraphy markets in Jinan.

🎋 Weifang Kite Craft

Though centered in Weifang, traditional Shandong kite-making is celebrated across the province, including in Jinan.

Where to find it: Cultural festivals and craft markets in Jinan.

🎭 Zaozhuago Willow Weaving

Traditional basket weaving using local willow, adapted for decorative and household items.

Where to find it: Local craft markets and cultural fairs.

🎶 Shandong Fast Tune (Shandong Qinshu)

A traditional storytelling art accompanied by string instruments, popular in older Shandong communities.

Where to find it: Community cultural centers and folk festivals.

Best Time to Visit Jinan

Plan your trip around Jinan's seasons for the best experience.

🌸

Spring (Apr–May)

12–22°C · Mild & Blooming

Pleasant temperatures and spring water flowing strongly. The willows along Daming Lake turn green.

☀️

Summer (Jun–Aug)

24–34°C · Hot & Humid

Hot and humid, but the springs and lakes provide relief. Early mornings and evenings are best for outdoor sightseeing.

🍂

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

12–24°C · Cool & Clear

The best time to visit. Clear skies, comfortable weather, and beautiful autumn colors on Thousand Buddha Mountain.

❄️

Winter (Dec–Feb)

-2–8°C · Cold & Dry

Cold and quiet, but the springs steam slightly in the cold air. Museums and hot pot restaurants are cozy escapes.
