Name Origin

The name "Hangzhou" (杭州) means "Boat Prefecture," referring to its location on the Qiantang River. The city was founded in 221 BC and reached its golden age during the Southern Song Dynasty (1127–1279) when it became the capital. Known as Lin'an at the time, it was the world's largest city with a population of over a million. Marco Polo visited in the 13th century and described it as "the finest and most splendid city in the world."

Education & Universities

Hangzhou is home to Zhejiang University (founded 1897, ranked top 3 in China), one of the country's most prestigious institutions. The city is also the headquarters of Alibaba, China's tech giant, and has become a major hub for innovation and entrepreneurship. Other notable universities include the China Academy of Art and Zhejiang University of Technology. Hangzhou's universities produce some of China's top tech talent.

Metro & Transportation

Hangzhou's metro system has 12 lines covering 500+ km, making it one of China's most efficient. The airport line connects Xiaoshan Airport (HGH) to the city center in 30 minutes. However, the Longjing tea villages and Lingyin Temple are not reachable by metro. Our private car service is the most convenient way to explore the scenic areas around West Lake, especially with a driver who knows the best viewpoints and tea houses.

Natural Resources & Environment

Hangzhou sits on the banks of the Qiantang River, with the iconic West Lake at its center. The surrounding hills are covered with Longjing (Dragon Well) tea plantations, producing China's most famous green tea. The city is surrounded by the Tianmu Mountains, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. Hangzhou's climate is humid subtropical with mild winters and hot summers. The city is renowned for its natural beauty, with over 60% green coverage.

Top Hangzhou Attractions

Discover Marco Polo's "Heaven on Earth" with a private driver.

🌊

West Lake (Xi Hu)

UNESCO World Heritage • Iconic Lake

The symbol of Hangzhou and one of China's most famous scenic spots. The lake features the Ten Scenes of West Lake, including the Su Causeway, Broken Bridge, and Three Pools Mirroring the Moon. A boat ride on the lake is a must. Your driver can drop you at the lakeside and pick you up after your exploration.

🍵

Longjing Tea Villages

Dragon Well Tea • Tea Plantations

The home of Longjing (Dragon Well) tea, China's most famous green tea. The villages of Longjing, Meijiawu, and Manjuelong are nestled in the hills west of West Lake. Visit a tea plantation, watch the tea-making process, and enjoy a cup of freshly brewed tea. Your driver knows the best family-run tea houses away from tourist crowds.

⛩️

Lingyin Temple

1,600 Years Old • Buddhist Temple

One of the wealthiest and most famous Buddhist temples in China, founded in 326 AD. The temple complex features magnificent halls, pagodas, and over 300 stone carvings on the Feilai Feng (Flying Peak). The surrounding forest is peaceful and lush. Your driver can drop you at the entrance and pick you up after your visit.

🏯

Six Harmonies Pagoda (Liuhe Ta)

Song Dynasty • Qiantang River View

A towering pagoda built in 970 AD on the banks of the Qiantang River. Climb to the top for panoramic views of the river, the city, and the surrounding hills. The nearby Qiantang River Tidal Bore is a spectacular natural phenomenon, especially during the Mid-Autumn Festival.

🌿

Xixi National Wetland Park

China's First National Wetland Park

A vast urban wetland park with waterways, marshes, and traditional villages. Take a boat ride through the waterways, visit the old fishing villages, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere. The park is especially beautiful in spring when the plum blossoms are in full bloom. Your driver can arrange a full day of exploration.

🏛️

Hefang Street

Historic Pedestrian Street • Qing Dynasty

A historic pedestrian street near West Lake, filled with traditional shops, tea houses, pharmacies, and restaurants. The street dates back to the Southern Song Dynasty and has been beautifully restored. Perfect for souvenir shopping and sampling local snacks. Your driver can drop you at the entrance and pick you up after your stroll.

Getting to Hangzhou: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Hangzhou and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport (HGH)

East of the city · 30 km to West Lake

HGH is Hangzhou's main airport and one of eastern China's busiest. It has three terminals: Terminal 1 (domestic), Terminal 2 (domestic and international), and Terminal 4 (newest, mainly international). The airport metro line connects to the city center in 30 minutes, but with luggage a private car is far more comfortable. By road, expect 40–50 minutes to West Lake or Wulin Square.

Hangzhou Xiaoshan Airport (Future Expansion)

Growing capacity · New runways planned

The airport is expanding to serve Hangzhou's growing role as a tech and tourism hub. Current terminals handle flights to most major Asian cities and European hubs. Our drivers monitor terminal assignments and will meet you at the correct arrival hall.

🚄 Railway Stations

Hangzhou East Station

High-speed hub · 20 min to West Lake

The main station for high-speed trains to Shanghai (1 hour), Beijing (4.5 hours), Nanjing, and Suzhou. It's one of China's largest railway stations. A private car pickup is recommended as the station is vast and taxi queues can be long.

Hangzhou Station (City Station)

Central station · 10 min to West Lake

Located near the city center, this station serves conventional trains to southern and western China. Its central location makes it convenient for hotels near West Lake and Wulin Square.

Hangzhou South Station

Reopened 2020 · Xiaoshan District

Reopened after major renovation, this station mainly serves high-speed trains on the Shanghai–Kunming line. It's convenient for travelers staying in the Xiaoshan district or visiting the airport.

Hangzhou West Station (New)

Newest station · Yuhang District

Opened in 2022, this modern station serves high-speed trains to western China and is part of the new westward expansion of Hangzhou. It's convenient for the Yuhang district and future tech zone developments.

Jiashan South Station (for Xitang)

For Xitang Water Town · 80 km northeast

If you're visiting the beautiful Xitang Water Town, you can take the high-speed train to Jiashan South Station. Our driver can also take you directly from Hangzhou in about 1.5 hours.

The terraced tea fields of Meijiawu and Longjing village, draped in morning fog, offer serene landscape photography. The layered green hills with traditional village houses are especially beautiful in spring.

Food & Shopping in Hangzhou

🍽️ Must-Try Hangzhou Cuisine

Dongpo Pork (Dongpo Rou)

Hangzhou's most famous dish — tender pork belly braised in soy sauce and wine until meltingly soft. Named after the Song Dynasty poet Su Dongpo. The dish is rich, sweet, and deeply savory. Your driver can take you to the best restaurant serving authentic Dongpo Pork near West Lake.

West Lake Fish in Sweet & Sour Sauce (Xihu Cu Yu)

Fresh grass carp from West Lake, prepared with a sweet and sour sauce. The fish is served whole, with the head and tail intact. A Hangzhou specialty that dates back over 800 years. The best version is found at the historic Louwailou restaurant on Gushan Island.

Longjing Tea Shrimp (Longjing Xia Ren)

Fresh shrimp stir-fried with Longjing tea leaves. The tea adds a subtle fragrance and removes any fishiness. A delicate dish that perfectly captures Hangzhou's culinary philosophy of using local ingredients. Your driver knows the best lakeside restaurant for this dish.

Beggar's Chicken (Jiao Hua Ji)

A whole chicken stuffed with herbs and mushrooms, wrapped in lotus leaves and clay, then baked. The legend says a beggar stole a chicken and cooked it this way. The result is incredibly tender and fragrant. A dramatic dish that your driver can help you order at the right restaurant.

Hefang Street

A historic pedestrian street near West Lake, filled with traditional shops, tea houses, pharmacies, and restaurants. Perfect for silk, tea, and traditional Chinese medicine. The street dates back to the Southern Song Dynasty and has been beautifully restored.

Buy authentic Longjing tea directly from the growers in the tea villages west of West Lake. Your driver can take you to the best family-run tea houses where you can taste before you buy. The spring harvest (April) is the most prized.

Hangzhou has been China's silk capital for over 1,000 years. The Xinhua Silk Factory offers high-quality silk products at reasonable prices. Your driver can take you to the factory showroom where you can watch silk-making demonstrations and purchase genuine silk scarves, clothing, and bedding.

🎁 What to Buy in Hangzhou

Longjing (Dragon Well) Tea

Hangzhou's most famous product — China's premier green tea. Buy directly from the tea villages (Meijiawu, Longjing, Manjuelong) for the freshest spring harvest. The pre-Qingming (Mingqian) tea picked before April 5th is the most prized. Your driver can take you to family-run tea houses for tasting.

Hangzhou Silk

Hangzhou has been China's silk capital for over 1,000 years. The Xinhua Silk Factory and China Silk Museum offer high-quality silk scarves, clothing, and bedding. Watch silk-making demonstrations and purchase genuine silk products. Your driver can take you to the factory showroom.

West Lake Silk Parasols

These elegant parasols, made with bamboo frames and silk canopies, are a Hangzhou specialty. Hand-painted with scenes of West Lake, they make beautiful decorative items. Find them at the Silk Museum or Hefang Street craft shops.

Hangzhou Fans (Wangxingji)

The Wangxingji fan shop has been making traditional folding fans since 1875. Crafted from sandalwood, bamboo, and silk, these fans are both functional and artistic. The black paper fans with gold paintings are especially prized.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Hangzhou

Meet the traditional arts that have defined Hangzhou for centuries — and the artisans keeping them alive.

🍵 Longjing Tea Processing

UNESCO-listed intangible heritage. The traditional processing of Longjing (Dragon Well) tea involves ten precise steps: withering, rolling, pan-frying, and shaping by hand. Master tea makers use their bare hands in hot woks to create the flat, jade-green leaves that define this world-famous tea.

Where to experience it: Longjing Tea Village or the China Tea Museum. Ask your driver to arrange a tea-making demonstration.

🧵 Hangzhou Silk Weaving

Hangzhou has been China's silk capital for over 1,000 years. The traditional silk weaving process involves reeling, throwing, dyeing, and weaving on wooden looms. The famous "cloud brocade" technique creates fabrics with intricate patterns that seem to float.

Where to find it: China Silk Museum or the Xinhua Silk Factory showroom. Demonstrations are available.

🎨 Hangzhou Fan Making (Wangxingji)

Founded in 1875, Wangxingji is one of China's most famous fan makers. The traditional process involves over 80 steps, from selecting bamboo to painting the silk or paper surface. The black paper fans with gold paintings are especially prized.

Where to find it: Wangxingji Fan Shop on Hefang Street, or the Hangzhou Arts and Crafts Museum.

🎭 Yue Opera (Yueju)

A traditional opera style originating in the Shaoxing area near Hangzhou, Yue Opera is known for its lyrical, romantic style and all-female casts. The music is softer and more melodic than Peking Opera, with stories drawn from classical literature.

Where to experience it: Hangzhou Yue Opera Theatre or the Zhejiang Xiaobaihua Yue Opera Troupe.

🎋 Bamboo Weaving (Xiaoshan)

The Xiaoshan area near Hangzhou is famous for its intricate bamboo weaving. Master craftsmen create everything from baskets and furniture to delicate decorative items using techniques passed down for centuries. The "double-layer" weaving technique is particularly prized.

Where to find it: Hangzhou Arts and Crafts Museum, or select shops on Hefang Street.

🖌️ Zhejiang School of Painting (Zhepai)

A distinctive painting style that emerged in Hangzhou during the Ming Dynasty, blending traditional Chinese brush techniques with local landscape subjects. Zhepai artists are known for their bold, expressive brushwork and focus on the natural beauty of the Jiangnan region.

Where to find it: Zhejiang Art Museum, Xiling Seal Art Society, and galleries on Hefang Street.

Walk the Su Causeway at Dawn

The Su Causeway is packed by 9 AM. Arrive at 6 AM to see locals practicing tai chi, fishermen casting nets, and the morning mist over the willow-lined causeway. It's the most peaceful time at West Lake.

Buy Tea Direct from the Village

Skip the tourist shops and go to Meijiawu or Longjing Village to buy tea directly from the growers. You'll get fresher tea at better prices, plus a free tea-tasting session. Your driver knows the best family-run farms.

Hangzhou's public bike system is excellent. Rent a bike and cycle around the lake — it's flat, scenic, and the bike lanes are well-maintained. The 15 km loop takes about 2 hours with stops for photos.

Best Time to Visit Hangzhou

Heaven on Earth is beautiful in every season.

🌸

Spring

Mar–May · 12–22°C

Mild and blooming. Peach blossoms at Quyuan and willow trees along the lake. The most poetic season for West Lake.

Highlights: Longjing tea picking, flower festivals

☀️

Summer

Jun–Aug · 25–35°C

Hot and humid with lotus flowers in full bloom. Evening lake cruises and mountain retreats offer relief from the heat.

Highlights: Lotus viewing, Qiandao Lake swimming

🍂

Autumn

Sep–Nov · 15–25°C

The best season — osmanthus flowers perfume the city, and the Mid-Autumn Festival moon over West Lake is legendary.

Highlights: Osmanthus season, Mid-Autumn Festival

❄️

Winter

Dec–Feb · 2–10°C

Cold and damp with occasional snow. The famous "Snow on the Broken Bridge" is a classic West Lake scene. Fewer tourists.

Highlights: Snow scenery, hot Longjing tea
