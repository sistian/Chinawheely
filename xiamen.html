Key Fact
About Xiamen

Name Origin

The name Xiamen (厦门) means 'Mansion Gate' and was first used during the Ming Dynasty. The city sits on the southeastern coast of Fujian, facing Taiwan across the Taiwan Strait. Its history as a treaty port and emigration hub has given it a cosmopolitan, outward-looking character.

Education & Universities

Xiamen is home to Xiamen University, one of China's most beautiful campuses, ranked among the top universities nationwide. Jimei University and Huaqiao University also have strong reputations, especially for overseas Chinese studies and marine science.

Metro & Transportation

Xiamen Metro has 3 lines connecting the airport, railway stations, and main districts. The city is spread across islands and coastline, making a private car ideal for visiting Gulangyu ferry terminals, the Hulishan Fortress, and coastal viewpoints efficiently.

Natural Resources & Environment

Xiamen is located on Xiamen Island and the surrounding mainland coast, with a subtropical climate and abundant greenery. The city is famous for its clean air, beaches, and the car-free Gulangyu Island, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Top Xiamen Attractions

Discover Xiamen's most memorable sights with a private driver.

🏝️

Gulangyu Island

UNESCO Island • Colonial Villas

A car-free island filled with colonial-era villas, gardens, piano museums, and narrow lanes. The ferry ride from Xiamen takes just a few minutes. Plan at least half a day.

🙏

Nanputuo Temple

Buddhist Temple • Wulao Peak

A famous Buddhist temple at the foot of Wulao Peak, near Xiamen University. The temple is free to enter, and hiking to the summit offers panoramic views of the city and sea.

🏰

Hulishan Fortress

Coastal Defense • Cannon

A historic coastal fortress built in the late 1800s to defend against foreign ships. The huge Krupp cannon is one of the largest and best-preserved antique cannons in the world.

🌉

Yanwu Bridge & Coastal Road

Sea Views • Sunset

A scenic elevated coastal highway connecting Xiamen Island to the mainland. The views of the sea, islands, and city at sunset are spectacular.

🏛️

Xiamen University

Campus • Architecture

Often ranked as China's most beautiful university, with a lake, palm trees, and traditional architecture. Advance registration is required for visitors.

🌳

Zhongshan Road Pedestrian Street

Shopping • Colonial Architecture

A historic pedestrian street lined with arcades and colonial-style buildings. Great for shopping, snacks, and people-watching in the evening.

Getting to Xiamen: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Xiamen and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport (XMN)

North of Xiamen Island · 15 km to downtown

XMN is a modern airport serving domestic and international flights. It is located close to downtown, making transfers quick. Metro and airport buses are available, but a private car is the easiest way to reach hotels, especially after a long flight.

Nearby Airports

Quanzhou Jinjiang, Zhangzhou

Quanzhou Jinjiang Airport (JJN) is about 80 km north and serves as an alternative for some domestic routes. Zhangzhou does not have a major airport nearby.

🚄 Railway Stations

Xiamen Railway Station

Xiamen Island · City center

The main station on Xiamen Island serving high-speed trains to Fuzhou, Shenzhen, and Hangzhou. It is convenient for downtown hotels and connected to the metro.

Xiamen North Railway Station

Mainland Jimei district · 25 km north

A major high-speed rail hub with long-distance trains to Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen. The metro connects to the island in about 40 minutes. A private car is more comfortable with luggage.

Gaoqi Railway Station

Near airport · Suburban

A smaller station near the airport serving some conventional and intercity trains. Useful for travelers connecting directly from the airport area.

Jimei Railway Station

Jimei district · University area

Serves the Jimei district and nearby university campuses. Convenient for travelers visiting Jimei University or the Tan Kah Kee Memorial.

Tong'an Railway Station

Tong'an district · Suburban

Serves the eastern Tong'an district with some high-speed and conventional trains. Mainly used by local commuters.

Capture Xiamen from the most photogenic angles.

Best in early morning

Best on sunny afternoons

Best at golden hour

The rocky coastline near Hulishan Fortress, with waves crashing against the rocks and the old fortress in the background, makes for dramatic seascapes.

Best at night

The colonial arcades of Zhongshan Road are beautifully lit in the evening. The mix of old architecture and modern neon is visually striking.

From the summit above Nanputuo Temple, you can photograph the entire Xiamen skyline, the sea, and distant islands. Clear winter days offer the best visibility.

Food & Shopping in Xiamen

🍽️ Must-Try Xiamen Cuisine

Oyster Omelette (Hézǎi Jiān)

A Fujian and Taiwanese street-food classic made with fresh oysters, eggs, sweet potato starch, and greens. Crispy on the edges and tender in the center. Found throughout Xiamen.

Satay Noodles (Shāchá Miàn)

Wheat noodles in a rich, nutty satay sauce with shrimp, pork, and vegetables. It's a beloved local breakfast and comfort food in Xiamen.

Fish Balls (Yúwán)

Springy fish balls with savory pork filling, served in a clear soup. Xiamen's version is famous for its bouncy texture and fresh seafood flavor.

Peanut Soup (Huāshēng Tāng)

A sweet, creamy soup made from slow-cooked peanuts. It's a traditional Xiamen breakfast item, often served with fried dough sticks.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Zhongshan Road

Xiamen's main historic shopping street, with arcades, boutiques, snack shops, and souvenir stores. Lively in the evening and close to the ferry terminal.

Gulangyu Island Shops

The narrow lanes of Gulangyu are filled with artisan shops, cafes, music-themed souvenirs, and handmade crafts. Great for unique gifts and local snacks.

SM City Xiamen

One of the largest shopping malls in the city, with international brands, restaurants, and entertainment. Located near the airport, convenient for last-minute shopping.

Jimei Night Market

A local night market near Jimei University with affordable street food, clothing, and accessories. A fun place to experience student life and local snacks.

🎁 What to Buy in Xiamen

Fujian Oolong Tea

Fujian is the birthplace of oolong tea. Tieguanyin, Dahongpao, and local Xiamen blends make excellent souvenirs. Buy from reputable tea shops in the city center.

Gulangyu Music Box

Gulangyu is known as the 'Island of Music.' Music boxes, piano-themed souvenirs, and local recordings are popular gifts.

Dried Seafood

Xiamen is a seafood city, and dried shrimp, squid, fish floss, and seaweed are widely sold. Choose vacuum-packed products for travel.

Lacquerware & Bamboo Crafts

Fujian has a long tradition of lacquerware and bamboo weaving. Small decorative items and jewelry are available in local craft shops.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Xiamen

Meet the traditional arts that have shaped Xiamen's identity for centuries.

🏮 Nanputuo Temple Vegetarian Cuisine

The temple's vegetarian restaurant has served Buddhist vegetarian dishes for over a century. The cooking technique is recognized as intangible cultural heritage.

Where to find it: Nanputuo Temple vegetarian restaurant, especially during Buddhist festivals.

🎵 Gulangyu Piano Culture

Gulangyu has produced many famous pianists and has a strong Western classical music tradition. The island's piano museum and frequent concerts keep this heritage alive.

Where to find it: Gulangyu Piano Museum and outdoor concerts during the Xiamen Music Festival.

🎭 Liyuan Opera (Lǐyuán Xì)

A traditional Fujian opera form with ancient roots, featuring distinctive music, costumes, and makeup. It is one of the oldest opera genres in China.

Where to find it: Traditional teahouses and cultural performances in Xiamen and nearby Quanzhou.

🧵 Xiamen Bead Embroidery

A delicate craft where tiny beads are embroidered onto fabric to create shimmering patterns. It is traditionally used for decorative clothing and accessories.

Where to find it: Gulangyu craft shops and local art markets on Zhongshan Road.

🏛️ Fujian Tulou Building Craft

The traditional rammed-earth construction of Fujian tulou is a UNESCO-listed craft. Some workshops near Xiamen offer demonstrations of this ancient building technique.

Where to find it: Tulou villages in Nanjing and Yongding counties, accessible on a day trip.

🎨 Hakka Blue Dyeing

A traditional indigo dyeing technique used by Hakka communities in Fujian. The deep blue cloth is used for clothing, bags, and home textiles.

Where to find it: Cultural villages and craft markets in Hakka areas near Xiamen.

Xiamen Local Secrets

Insider tips to help you experience Xiamen like a local.

🏝️

Stay Overnight on Gulangyu

Gulangyu is packed with day-trippers from 10 AM to 4 PM. Stay overnight and enjoy the island's quiet mornings and romantic evenings.

🎟️

Book Xiamen University in Advance

The university requires online reservation for visitors. Book several days ahead, especially on weekends, or you'll be turned away.

🍜

Eat Shacha Noodles for Breakfast

Satay noodles are a local breakfast staple. Look for small family-run shops near Zhongshan Road or the university area.

🚗

Use a Private Car for Tulou Trips

Fujian Tulou is 2.5 hours away. A private car is far more comfortable than public buses and lets you visit multiple sites in one day.

🌅

Watch Sunset from Hulishan

The rocky coast near Hulishan Fortress is a beautiful sunset spot. Arrive an hour before sunset to find a good position.

🎵

Catch a Free Outdoor Concert

Gulangyu often hosts free or low-cost outdoor concerts in the summer. Check the local event listings for music under the stars.

Best Time to Visit Xiamen

Plan your trip around Xiamen's seasons for the best experience.

🌸

Spring (Mar–May)

18–26°C · Warm & Humid

A pleasant time to visit Xiamen with blooming flowers and mild temperatures. Occasional rain showers, but the city is green and beautiful.

☀️

Summer (Jun–Aug)

26–33°C · Hot & Typhoon Season

Hot and humid with possible typhoons. Beaches are popular, but crowds and rain can be an issue. Check weather forecasts.

🍂

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

22–30°C · Warm & Dry

The best season for Xiamen. Warm, dry weather, clear skies, and fewer typhoons. Perfect for Gulangyu, beaches, and coastal drives.

❄️

Winter (Dec–Feb)

10–18°C · Mild & Cool

Mild and comfortable compared to northern China. Fewer crowds and lower prices. Good for city sightseeing and temple visits. Swimming is too cold for most.

Useful Chinese Phrases for Xiamen

A few key phrases to help you navigate Xiamen.

你好 (Nǐ hǎo)

Hello / Hi

Basic greeting for meeting your driver and shopkeepers.

谢谢 (Xièxiè)

Thank you

Use after receiving help or service.

我要去鼓浪屿 (Wǒ yào qù Gǔlàngyǔ)

I want to go to Gulangyu

Useful for telling your driver your destination.

这个多少钱？ (Zhège duōshao qián?)

How much is this?

Useful for shopping at markets and souvenir shops.

一杯沙茶面 (Yì bēi shāchá miàn)

A bowl of satay noodles

A must-try local food phrase. Note: should be 'yì wǎn' but commonly used.

厕所在哪里？ (Cèsuǒ zài nǎlǐ?)

Where is the bathroom?

Essential when exploring tourist sites and night markets.
