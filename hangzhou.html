Name Origin

The name "Hangzhou" (杭州) means "Boat Prefecture," referring to its location on the Qiantang River. The city was founded in 221 BC and reached its golden age during the Southern Song Dynasty (1127–1279) when it became the capital. Known as Lin'an at the time, it was the world's largest city with a population of over a million. Marco Polo visited in the 13th century and described it as "the finest and most splendid city in the world."

Education & Universities

Hangzhou is home to Zhejiang University (founded 1897, ranked top 3 in China), one of the country's most prestigious institutions. The city is also the headquarters of Alibaba, China's tech giant, and has become a major hub for innovation and entrepreneurship. Other notable universities include the China Academy of Art and Zhejiang University of Technology. Hangzhou's universities produce some of China's top tech talent.

Metro & Transportation

Hangzhou's metro system has 12 lines covering 500+ km, making it one of China's most efficient. The airport line connects Xiaoshan Airport (HGH) to the city center in 30 minutes. However, the Longjing tea villages and Lingyin Temple are not reachable by metro. Our private car service is the most convenient way to explore the scenic areas around West Lake, especially with a driver who knows the best viewpoints and tea houses.

Natural Resources & Environment

Hangzhou sits on the banks of the Qiantang River, with the iconic West Lake at its center. The surrounding hills are covered with Longjing (Dragon Well) tea plantations, producing China's most famous green tea. The city is surrounded by the Tianmu Mountains, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. Hangzhou's climate is humid subtropical with mild winters and hot summers. The city is renowned for its natural beauty, with over 60% green coverage.

Top Hangzhou Attractions

Discover Marco Polo's "Heaven on Earth" with a private driver.

🌊

West Lake (Xi Hu)

UNESCO World Heritage • Iconic Lake

The symbol of Hangzhou and one of China's most famous scenic spots. The lake features the Ten Scenes of West Lake, including the Su Causeway, Broken Bridge, and Three Pools Mirroring the Moon. A boat ride on the lake is a must. Your driver can drop you at the lakeside and pick you up after your exploration.

🍵

Longjing Tea Villages

Dragon Well Tea • Tea Plantations

The home of Longjing (Dragon Well) tea, China's most famous green tea. The villages of Longjing, Meijiawu, and Manjuelong are nestled in the hills west of West Lake. Visit a tea plantation, watch the tea-making process, and enjoy a cup of freshly brewed tea. Your driver knows the best family-run tea houses away from tourist crowds.

⛩️

Lingyin Temple

1,600 Years Old • Buddhist Temple

One of the wealthiest and most famous Buddhist temples in China, founded in 326 AD. The temple complex features magnificent halls, pagodas, and over 300 stone carvings on the Feilai Feng (Flying Peak). The surrounding forest is peaceful and lush. Your driver can drop you at the entrance and pick you up after your visit.

🏯

Six Harmonies Pagoda (Liuhe Ta)

Song Dynasty • Qiantang River View

A towering pagoda built in 970 AD on the banks of the Qiantang River. Climb to the top for panoramic views of the river, the city, and the surrounding hills. The nearby Qiantang River Tidal Bore is a spectacular natural phenomenon, especially during the Mid-Autumn Festival.

🌿

Xixi National Wetland Park

China's First National Wetland Park

A vast urban wetland park with waterways, marshes, and traditional villages. Take a boat ride through the waterways, visit the old fishing villages, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere. The park is especially beautiful in spring when the plum blossoms are in full bloom. Your driver can arrange a full day of exploration.

🏛️

Hefang Street

Historic Pedestrian Street • Qing Dynasty

A historic pedestrian street near West Lake, filled with traditional shops, tea houses, pharmacies, and restaurants. The street dates back to the Southern Song Dynasty and has been beautifully restored. Perfect for souvenir shopping and sampling local snacks. Your driver can drop you at the entrance and pick you up after your stroll.

Food & Shopping in Hangzhou

🍽️ Must-Try Hangzhou Cuisine

Dongpo Pork (Dongpo Rou)

Hangzhou's most famous dish — tender pork belly braised in soy sauce and wine until meltingly soft. Named after the Song Dynasty poet Su Dongpo. The dish is rich, sweet, and deeply savory. Your driver can take you to the best restaurant serving authentic Dongpo Pork near West Lake.

West Lake Fish in Sweet & Sour Sauce (Xihu Cu Yu)

Fresh grass carp from West Lake, prepared with a sweet and sour sauce. The fish is served whole, with the head and tail intact. A Hangzhou specialty that dates back over 800 years. The best version is found at the historic Louwailou restaurant on Gushan Island.

Longjing Tea Shrimp (Longjing Xia Ren)

Fresh shrimp stir-fried with Longjing tea leaves. The tea adds a subtle fragrance and removes any fishiness. A delicate dish that perfectly captures Hangzhou's culinary philosophy of using local ingredients. Your driver knows the best lakeside restaurant for this dish.

Beggar's Chicken (Jiao Hua Ji)

A whole chicken stuffed with herbs and mushrooms, wrapped in lotus leaves and clay, then baked. The legend says a beggar stole a chicken and cooked it this way. The result is incredibly tender and fragrant. A dramatic dish that your driver can help you order at the right restaurant.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Hefang Street

A historic pedestrian street near West Lake, filled with traditional shops, tea houses, pharmacies, and restaurants. Perfect for silk, tea, and traditional Chinese medicine. The street dates back to the Southern Song Dynasty and has been beautifully restored.

Longjing Tea Village Shops

Buy authentic Longjing tea directly from the growers in the tea villages west of West Lake. Your driver can take you to the best family-run tea houses where you can taste before you buy. The spring harvest (April) is the most prized.

Silk Market (Xinhua Silk Factory)

Hangzhou has been China's silk capital for over 1,000 years. The Xinhua Silk Factory offers high-quality silk products at reasonable prices. Your driver can take you to the factory showroom where you can watch silk-making demonstrations and purchase genuine silk scarves, clothing, and bedding.

Wulin Square & Hangzhou Tower

Hangzhou's premier shopping district with luxury brands, international boutiques, and gourmet restaurants. Hangzhou Tower is the city's most upscale department store. Your driver can drop you off and pick you up after a day of shopping.
