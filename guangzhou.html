Name Origin

The name "Guangzhou" (广州) means "Broad Prefecture." The city was also known as Canton (from the Portuguese "Cantão"), which gave its name to Cantonese cuisine, Canton Fair, and the Cantonese language. Founded over 2,200 years ago during the Qin Dynasty, it has been a major trading port since the Han Dynasty. The Silk Road started here by sea, and it was one of China's first ports opened to foreign trade in the 18th century.

Education & Universities

Guangzhou is home to Sun Yat-sen University (founded 1924, ranked top 10 in China), one of the country's most prestigious institutions. South China University of Technology and Jinan University (founded 1906, one of China's oldest) are also highly regarded. The city's universities excel in medicine, business, and engineering. Guangzhou is also a major center for international education with many foreign students.

Metro & Transportation

Guangzhou's metro system has 16 lines covering 600+ km, one of China's busiest. The airport line connects Baiyun Airport (CAN) to the city center in 30 minutes. The city is also a major hub for China's high-speed rail network, with direct connections to Hong Kong (1 hour), Shenzhen (30 minutes), and Beijing (8 hours). However, during the Canton Fair (April and October), the metro is extremely crowded — a private car is essential for business travelers.

Natural Resources & Environment

Guangzhou sits on the Pearl River Delta, the most fertile and productive region in southern China. The Pearl River (Zhujiang) flows through the city, creating a network of waterways that have shaped the city's development for 2,000 years. The city has a humid subtropical climate with hot summers and mild winters. Baiyun Mountain (White Cloud Mountain) rises on the northern edge of the city, offering a green escape from the urban bustle.

Top Guangzhou Attractions

Discover the best of Cantonese culture with a private driver.

🗼

Canton Tower

600m Tall • Pearl River

One of the world's tallest towers at 600 meters, offering panoramic views of the Pearl River Delta. The tower features a revolving restaurant, observation deck, and a skywalk. At night, it's illuminated with a spectacular LED light show. Your driver can arrange an evening visit with a riverside dinner.

🏛️

Shamian Island

Colonial District • European Architecture

A former British and French concession with well-preserved colonial architecture from the 19th century. Tree-lined streets, Victorian buildings, and a peaceful atmosphere make it a unique oasis in the bustling city. Your driver can drop you off for a leisurely stroll and pick you up after lunch at one of the charming riverside cafés.

🕌

Chen Clan Ancestral Hall

Qing Dynasty • Folk Art Museum

A magnificent Qing Dynasty complex built in 1894, showcasing the finest examples of Cantonese decorative arts including wood carving, brick carving, and ceramic sculpture. Now a folk art museum, it's considered the best-preserved traditional architecture in Guangdong Province. Your driver can combine this with a visit to the nearby Liwan Lake.

🌳

Baiyun Mountain

White Cloud Mountain • Nature Escape

Known as the "City's Lung," Baiyun Mountain rises on the northern edge of Guangzhou, offering hiking trails, temples, and panoramic views of the city. The cable car provides easy access to the summit. Your driver can take you to the entrance and wait while you explore the mountain's scenic spots.

🛍️

Canton Fair (Pazhou Complex)

World's Largest Trade Fair • Twice Yearly

The China Import and Export Fair, known as the Canton Fair, is the world's largest trade fair held every April and October. Over 25,000 exhibitors and 200,000 buyers attend. Our private car service is the most reliable transportation during the fair, with drivers who know the Pazhou Complex well and can avoid traffic.

🌃

Pearl River Night Cruise

Night Cruise • Skyline Views

A night cruise along the Pearl River offers the best views of Guangzhou's illuminated skyline, including the Canton Tower, Liede Bridge, and the historic buildings along the waterfront. Your driver can drop you at the Tianzi Wharf and pick you up after the cruise for a seamless evening experience.

Getting to Guangzhou: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Guangzhou and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN)

North of the city · 30 km to Canton Tower

CAN is one of China's busiest airports and the main hub for southern China. It has two main terminals: Terminal 1 (domestic flights) and Terminal 2 (international and some domestic flights, opened in 2018). The airport metro line connects to the city center in 30 minutes, but with luggage a private car is far more comfortable. By road, expect 40–60 minutes to Tianhe or Yuexiu districts.

Guangzhou Baiyun Airport (Future Expansion)

Growing capacity · Third terminal planned

Baiyun Airport is expanding rapidly to serve the Greater Bay Area. The existing terminals handle flights to Southeast Asia, Europe, Australia, and Africa. During the Canton Fair (April and October), the airport is extremely busy — pre-booking a private car is essential.

🚄 Railway Stations

Guangzhou South Station

High-speed hub · 40 min to Canton Tower

The main station for high-speed trains to Shenzhen (30 min), Hong Kong (1 hour), Beijing (8 hours), and Wuhan (4 hours). It's one of Asia's largest stations. A private car pickup is strongly recommended during Canton Fair.

Guangzhou East Station

Central station · 15 min to Tianhe CBD

Located in the Tianhe district, this station serves conventional trains to eastern and northern China, as well as direct trains to Hong Kong (Hung Hom). Its central location makes it convenient for business travelers staying in Tianhe.

Guangzhou Station

Historic main station · Yuexiu District

The city's oldest main station, serving conventional trains to western and northern China. It's located near the city center and is convenient for hotels in Yuexiu and Liwan districts.

Guangzhou North Station

Regional trains · Huadu District

Mainly serves regional trains to northern Guangdong and connections to the airport. Less frequently used by tourists but useful for certain regional routes.

Panyu Station

Suburban HSR · Near Chimelong

Serves high-speed trains on the Guangzhou–Shenzhen–Hong Kong line. Convenient for travelers visiting Chimelong Paradise or staying in the Panyu district.

The tree-lined avenues and European-style buildings of the former British and French concession offer a unique colonial atmosphere. The white facades and green shutters are especially photogenic in morning light.

The illuminated skyline from a river cruise offers the best panoramic views of Guangzhou. The Liede Bridge, Canton Tower, and riverside skyscrapers create a dazzling cityscape.

🌳 Baiyun Mountain Summit

Best at sunrise

Known as the "City's Lung," Baiyun Mountain offers panoramic views of Guangzhou from its summit. On clear days, you can see the entire Pearl River Delta. The morning mist adds a mystical quality.

Best in soft afternoon light

Best at blue hour

The modern cable-stayed bridge over the Pearl River is beautifully illuminated at night. The reflection in the calm water, with the CBD skyline behind it, makes for a stunning urban landscape shot.

Food & Shopping in Guangzhou

🍽️ Must-Try Guangzhou Cuisine

Dim Sum (Yum Cha)

The heart of Cantonese cuisine. Small plates of steamed dumplings, BBQ pork buns, rice rolls, and egg tarts served with tea. Guangzhou is the birthplace of Dim Sum. Your driver can take you to the best traditional teahouse for an authentic Yum Cha experience.

Char Siu (BBQ Pork)

Tender, sweet, glazed pork roasted to perfection. The Cantonese signature dish. The best Char Siu is found in traditional BBQ shops in the old neighborhoods. Your driver knows the best spots that tourists rarely find.

White Cut Chicken (Bai Qie Ji)

Poached chicken served with ginger-scallion sauce. A Cantonese classic that highlights the freshness of the ingredients. Simple, elegant, and delicious. The best version is found in the traditional restaurants of Yuexiu District.

Double-Skin Milk (Shuang Pi Nai)

A famous Cantonese dessert made from buffalo milk, sugar, and egg white. Creamy, silky, and lightly sweet. The most famous shop is on Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street. Your driver can take you there for a sweet ending to your day.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street

Guangzhou's most famous shopping street in the historic Liwan District. A pedestrian area filled with traditional shops, street food, and bargain clothing. The nearby Qingping Market is one of China's largest traditional medicine markets.

Beijing Road

A modern shopping district with international brands, department stores, and restaurants. The pedestrian area has ancient Song Dynasty road remains visible beneath glass panels. Your driver can drop you at the main entrance.

Tianhe CBD

Guangzhou's modern business district with high-end shopping malls like TeeMall and Grandview Mall. Features luxury brands, international dining, and entertainment. Perfect for a sophisticated shopping experience.

Canton Fair Complex (Pazhou)

The world's largest exhibition center, hosting the Canton Fair twice yearly. Even outside fair periods, the area has numerous wholesale markets for textiles, electronics, and home goods. Your driver can navigate the complex's massive parking areas.

🎁 What to Buy in Guangzhou

Cantonese Embroidery (Yue Xiu)

One of China's four great embroidery styles, Cantonese embroidery is known for its vivid colors, three-dimensional effects, and use of gold thread. Traditional motifs include dragons, phoenixes, and flowers. Find it at the Guangdong Folk Art Museum or specialty shops on Shangxiajiu.

Duan Inkstones

The Duan inkstone, produced in nearby Zhaoqing, is one of China's four famous inkstones. Made from purple stone quarried from the Duanxi River, these inkstones are prized by calligraphers for their smooth texture. A beautiful and functional souvenir for art lovers.

Dried Seafood & Tonic Foods

Guangzhou's Qingping Market is famous for dried seafood, bird's nest, ginseng, and traditional tonic ingredients. These are deeply rooted in Cantonese culinary culture. Your driver can help you navigate the market and find reputable vendors.

Ivory-Colored Porcelain (Dehua)

Though originating in Fujian, Dehua's famous "blanc de Chine" porcelain is widely available in Guangzhou. The translucent white ceramic is used for delicate figurines, teaware, and decorative items. Look for pieces at the Guangzhou Museum shop or reputable porcelain dealers.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Guangzhou

Meet the traditional arts that have defined Guangzhou for centuries — and the artisans keeping them alive.

🎭 Cantonese Opera (Yueju)

UNESCO-listed intangible heritage. Cantonese Opera is one of China's major opera genres, known for its elaborate costumes, face painting, and unique singing style. It combines martial arts, acrobatics, and music, with stories drawn from Chinese history and folklore.

Where to experience it: Guangzhou Opera House, Hong Xian Nu Art Center, or the Lychee Bay Theatre. Ask your driver to book tickets.

🧵 Cantonese Embroidery (Yue Xiu)

One of China's four great embroidery styles, Cantonese embroidery is known for its vivid colors, three-dimensional effects, and extensive use of gold and silver thread. The technique originated in the Pearl River Delta over 1,000 years ago.

Where to find it: Guangdong Folk Art Museum, Chen Clan Ancestral Hall, or specialty embroidery shops on Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street.

🎨 Ivory Carving (Guangzhou Style)

Guangzhou has been a center for ivory carving for centuries, with artisans creating intricate balls-within-balls, miniature boats, and detailed figurines. Note: Due to international regulations, modern carvings use mammoth ivory or synthetic materials. The skill and artistry remain extraordinary.

Where to find it: Guangzhou Museum and select cultural heritage shops. Always verify the material source.

🎋 Canton Enamel (Guangcai)

A distinctive style of porcelain enamel decoration that developed in Guangzhou during the Qing Dynasty. Guangcai features bright colors, heavy gold outlines, and Western-influenced designs, reflecting the city's role as a trading port. It was originally produced for export to Europe.

Where to find it: Guangzhou Museum, Chen Clan Ancestral Hall, and select antique shops in Liwan District.

🎤 Cantonese Music (Guangdong Yinyue)

A traditional instrumental music style featuring the erhu, pipa, yangqin, and gaohu. Cantonese music is characterized by its bright, lively melodies and complex ornamentation. It developed in the teahouses and theaters of Guangzhou in the late 19th century.

Where to experience it: Traditional teahouses in Liwan District, or during the Spring Festival temple fair at Chen Clan Ancestral Hall.

🪡 Woodblock New Year Prints (Foshan)

Though originating in nearby Foshan, this traditional art form is closely tied to Guangzhou culture. Hand-carved woodblocks are used to print colorful New Year images of door gods, auspicious animals, and scenes of prosperity. The technique has been practiced for over 500 years.

Where to find it: Foshan Woodblock Print Museum (30 min from Guangzhou) or folk art shops in the old city.

Guangzhou Local Secrets

Insider tips to help you experience Guangzhou like a local.

🍵

Eat Dim Sum Like a Local

Skip the hotel breakfast and go to a traditional teahouse for yum cha (drinking tea). The best time is 8–10 AM on weekends when families gather. Try shrimp dumplings (har gow), BBQ pork buns (char siu bao), and egg tarts. Your driver knows the best old-school teahouses.

🌉

Walk the Pearl River at Night

The riverside promenade from Shamian Island to the Canton Tower is beautifully lit and surprisingly peaceful after 9 PM. The breeze from the river and the illuminated skyline make for a perfect evening stroll.

🛍️

Bargain at Qingping Market

The Qingping Medicine Market is a fascinating cultural experience. Start at 50% of the asking price and walk away if they don't budge. Your driver can help you negotiate and ensure you get authentic products, not tourist traps.

🌸

Seasonal Highlights

Spring: Kapok flowers along the Pearl River. Summer: Lotus at Lychee Bay. Autumn: Chrysanthemum shows at Baiyun Mountain. Winter: Warm claypot rice and ginger milk curd.

📅

Avoid Canton Fair Dates Unless Business

The Canton Fair (mid-April and mid-October) makes everything more expensive and crowded. Hotels triple their rates, and traffic is gridlocked. If you're not attending the fair, visit a week before or after.

🚗

Day Trip to Kaiping Diaolou

The UNESCO-listed fortified watchtowers of Kaiping are a 2-hour drive from Guangzhou. Your driver can take you through the countryside to explore these unique buildings, have lunch at a local village restaurant, and return by evening.

Best Time to Visit Guangzhou

The City of Flowers blooms year-round.

🌸

Spring

Mar–May · 18–28°C

Warm and humid with kapok flowers blooming. The most comfortable season for outdoor exploration and dim sum brunches.

Highlights: Flower markets, Pearl River cruises

☀️

Summer

Jun–Aug · 26–35°C

Hot, humid, and rainy with typhoon season. Air conditioning is essential. Indoor attractions and evening activities are best.

Highlights: Shopping malls, night markets, AC restaurants

🍂

Autumn

Sep–Nov · 20–30°C

The best season — warm, dry, and sunny. Perfect for walking tours, rooftop dining, and day trips. Avoid Canton Fair weeks.

Highlights: Baiyun Mountain hiking, outdoor dining

❄️

Winter

Dec–Feb · 10–20°C

Mild and pleasant — Guangzhou's winter is like spring elsewhere. Perfect for exploring without the summer heat.

Highlights: Chinese New Year flower fairs, claypot rice
