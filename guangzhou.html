Name Origin

The name "Guangzhou" (广州) means "Broad Prefecture." The city was also known as Canton (from the Portuguese "Cantão"), which gave its name to Cantonese cuisine, Canton Fair, and the Cantonese language. Founded over 2,200 years ago during the Qin Dynasty, it has been a major trading port since the Han Dynasty. The Silk Road started here by sea, and it was one of China's first ports opened to foreign trade in the 18th century.

Education & Universities

Guangzhou is home to Sun Yat-sen University (founded 1924, ranked top 10 in China), one of the country's most prestigious institutions. South China University of Technology and Jinan University (founded 1906, one of China's oldest) are also highly regarded. The city's universities excel in medicine, business, and engineering. Guangzhou is also a major center for international education with many foreign students.

Metro & Transportation

Guangzhou's metro system has 16 lines covering 600+ km, one of China's busiest. The airport line connects Baiyun Airport (CAN) to the city center in 30 minutes. The city is also a major hub for China's high-speed rail network, with direct connections to Hong Kong (1 hour), Shenzhen (30 minutes), and Beijing (8 hours). However, during the Canton Fair (April and October), the metro is extremely crowded — a private car is essential for business travelers.

Natural Resources & Environment

Guangzhou sits on the Pearl River Delta, the most fertile and productive region in southern China. The Pearl River (Zhujiang) flows through the city, creating a network of waterways that have shaped the city's development for 2,000 years. The city has a humid subtropical climate with hot summers and mild winters. Baiyun Mountain (White Cloud Mountain) rises on the northern edge of the city, offering a green escape from the urban bustle.

Top Guangzhou Attractions

Discover the best of Cantonese culture with a private driver.

🗼

Canton Tower

600m Tall • Pearl River

One of the world's tallest towers at 600 meters, offering panoramic views of the Pearl River Delta. The tower features a revolving restaurant, observation deck, and a skywalk. At night, it's illuminated with a spectacular LED light show. Your driver can arrange an evening visit with a riverside dinner.

🏛️

Shamian Island

Colonial District • European Architecture

A former British and French concession with well-preserved colonial architecture from the 19th century. Tree-lined streets, Victorian buildings, and a peaceful atmosphere make it a unique oasis in the bustling city. Your driver can drop you off for a leisurely stroll and pick you up after lunch at one of the charming riverside cafés.

🕌

Chen Clan Ancestral Hall

Qing Dynasty • Folk Art Museum

A magnificent Qing Dynasty complex built in 1894, showcasing the finest examples of Cantonese decorative arts including wood carving, brick carving, and ceramic sculpture. Now a folk art museum, it's considered the best-preserved traditional architecture in Guangdong Province. Your driver can combine this with a visit to the nearby Liwan Lake.

🌳

Baiyun Mountain

White Cloud Mountain • Nature Escape

Known as the "City's Lung," Baiyun Mountain rises on the northern edge of Guangzhou, offering hiking trails, temples, and panoramic views of the city. The cable car provides easy access to the summit. Your driver can take you to the entrance and wait while you explore the mountain's scenic spots.

🛍️

Canton Fair (Pazhou Complex)

World's Largest Trade Fair • Twice Yearly

The China Import and Export Fair, known as the Canton Fair, is the world's largest trade fair held every April and October. Over 25,000 exhibitors and 200,000 buyers attend. Our private car service is the most reliable transportation during the fair, with drivers who know the Pazhou Complex well and can avoid traffic.

🌃

Pearl River Night Cruise

Night Cruise • Skyline Views

A night cruise along the Pearl River offers the best views of Guangzhou's illuminated skyline, including the Canton Tower, Liede Bridge, and the historic buildings along the waterfront. Your driver can drop you at the Tianzi Wharf and pick you up after the cruise for a seamless evening experience.

Food & Shopping in Guangzhou

🍽️ Must-Try Guangzhou Cuisine

Dim Sum (Yum Cha)

The heart of Cantonese cuisine. Small plates of steamed dumplings, BBQ pork buns, rice rolls, and egg tarts served with tea. Guangzhou is the birthplace of Dim Sum. Your driver can take you to the best traditional teahouse for an authentic Yum Cha experience.

Char Siu (BBQ Pork)

Tender, sweet, glazed pork roasted to perfection. The Cantonese signature dish. The best Char Siu is found in traditional BBQ shops in the old neighborhoods. Your driver knows the best spots that tourists rarely find.

White Cut Chicken (Bai Qie Ji)

Poached chicken served with ginger-scallion sauce. A Cantonese classic that highlights the freshness of the ingredients. Simple, elegant, and delicious. The best version is found in the traditional restaurants of Yuexiu District.

Double-Skin Milk (Shuang Pi Nai)

A famous Cantonese dessert made from buffalo milk, sugar, and egg white. Creamy, silky, and lightly sweet. The most famous shop is on Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street. Your driver can take you there for a sweet ending to your day.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street

Guangzhou's most famous shopping street in the historic Liwan District. A pedestrian area filled with traditional shops, street food, and bargain clothing. The nearby Qingping Market is one of China's largest traditional medicine markets.

Beijing Road

A modern shopping district with international brands, department stores, and restaurants. The pedestrian area has ancient Song Dynasty road remains visible beneath glass panels. Your driver can drop you at the main entrance.

Tianhe CBD

Guangzhou's modern business district with high-end shopping malls like TeeMall and Grandview Mall. Features luxury brands, international dining, and entertainment. Perfect for a sophisticated shopping experience.

Canton Fair Complex (Pazhou)

The world's largest exhibition center, hosting the Canton Fair twice yearly. Even outside fair periods, the area has numerous wholesale markets for textiles, electronics, and home goods. Your driver can navigate the complex's massive parking areas.
