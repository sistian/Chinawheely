Name Origin

The name "Shanghai" (上海) literally means "Upon the Sea." The city began as a small fishing village during the Song Dynasty. In 1842, after the First Opium War, Shanghai became one of China's first treaty ports, opening to foreign trade. By the 1930s, it was Asia's most glamorous cosmopolitan city — known as the "Paris of the East" and the "Pearl of the Orient."

Education & Universities

Shanghai is home to Fudan University (founded 1905, ranked top 50 globally), Shanghai Jiao Tong University (founded 1896), and Tongji University (famous for architecture). The city also hosts NYU Shanghai, China's first Sino-US joint research university. Over 90 universities produce 200,000+ graduates annually.

Metro & Transportation

Shanghai boasts the world's longest metro network with 20 lines and 800+ km of track, handling 13 million daily passengers. The Maglev train connects Pudong Airport to downtown in 8 minutes at 430 km/h. Our private car service offers door-to-door convenience from both Pudong (PVG) and Hongqiao (SHA) airports.

Natural Resources & Environment

Shanghai sits at the mouth of the Yangtze River, the world's third-longest river. The Huangpu River divides the city into Pudong (east) and Puxi (west). Chongming Island at the river's mouth is the world's largest alluvial island. The city has invested heavily in green spaces including the Shanghai Botanical Garden and Century Park.

Top Shanghai Attractions

From colonial waterfront to futuristic skyline, discover Shanghai's most iconic sights.

🏙️

The Bund (Waitan)

Colonial Waterfront • Historic Landmark

Shanghai's most famous waterfront promenade, lined with grand colonial buildings from the 1920s–30s. The contrast between the historic Bund and the futuristic Pudong skyline across the river is breathtaking.

🌸

Yu Garden (Yuyuan)

Ming Dynasty Garden • Traditional Architecture

A 400-year-old classical Chinese garden built during the Ming Dynasty. Featuring pavilions, rockeries, ponds, and bridges. The adjacent Yuyuan Bazaar is perfect for souvenir shopping.

🗼

Oriental Pearl Tower

Lujiazui • Pudong Skyline

One of Pudong's most recognizable landmarks, offering panoramic views from its observation deck. The surrounding Lujiazui financial district is home to the Shanghai Tower, Jin Mao Tower, and Shanghai World Financial Center.

🏛️

French Concession (Xuhui)

Colonial District • Tree-Lined Streets

A former French colonial district with charming tree-lined streets, art deco buildings, boutique cafés, and designer shops. The area around Fuxing Road and Wukang Road is perfect for a leisurely stroll.

🏛️

Shanghai Museum

People's Square • Ancient Art Collection

One of China's finest museums, housing over 120,000 pieces of ancient Chinese art including bronze, ceramics, calligraphy, and jade. The building is designed to resemble an ancient Chinese bronze vessel.

🌃

Nanjing Road

Shopping Street • World's Longest

The world's longest shopping street at 5.5 km, stretching from the Bund to Jing'an Temple. Features everything from historic department stores to luxury boutiques and neon-lit pedestrian malls.

Getting to Shanghai: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Shanghai and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Shanghai Pudong International Airport (PVG)

East of the city · 45 km to The Bund

PVG is Shanghai's main international gateway and one of the world's busiest airports. It has two main terminals: Terminal 1 (mainly domestic and some international) and Terminal 2 (most international flights, Star Alliance and SkyTeam). The Maglev train connects to Longyang Road in 8 minutes at 430 km/h, but with luggage a private car is far more comfortable. By road, expect 50–70 minutes to The Bund or Lujiazui.

Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport (SHA)

West of the city · 15 km to People's Square

SHA is closer to downtown and mainly serves domestic flights, though some international routes operate here too. It has two terminals: Terminal 1 (domestic) and Terminal 2 (domestic and international). The airport is connected to the metro and Hongqiao Railway Station. A private car to downtown hotels typically takes 30–40 minutes.

🚄 Railway Stations

Shanghai Hongqiao Station

High-speed hub · 20 min to The Bund

The main station for high-speed trains to Beijing, Nanjing, Hangzhou, Suzhou, and most domestic destinations. It's one of Asia's largest railway stations. A private car pickup is recommended as the station is vast and taxi queues can be long.

Shanghai Station

Historic central station · 10 min to People's Square

Located near Suzhou Creek, this is Shanghai's oldest main station. It serves conventional trains to northern and western China, as well as some high-speed services. Its central location makes hotel transfers quick and convenient.

Shanghai South Station

Southern trains · Xuhui District

Mainly serves conventional trains to southern China including Zhejiang, Fujian, and Guangdong provinces. The station building has a distinctive circular design. It's convenient for hotels in the Xuhui and Minhang districts.

Shanghai West Station

Freight & regional · Putuo District

Primarily used for freight and some regional passenger services. Located in the Putuo district, it's less relevant for most travelers but useful for certain regional routes.

Songjiang Station

Suburban HSR · Near Thames Town

Serves high-speed trains on the Shanghai–Hangzhou line. Convenient for travelers staying in the Songjiang district or visiting the nearby ancient water towns like Zhujiajiao.

Shanghai's Best Photo Spots

Capture the city from the most iconic and photogenic angles.

🌃 The Bund at Sunrise

Best at dawn

The colonial facades of the Bund glow golden in the early morning light, with the Pudong skyline across the river as a dramatic backdrop. Arrive before 6 AM for the best light and almost no crowds.

🏙️ Lujiazui Skyline from the Bund

Best at blue hour

The classic Shanghai shot: the Oriental Pearl Tower, Shanghai Tower, and Jin Mao Tower reflected in the Huangpu River. The blue hour just after sunset offers the most magical colors.

🌸 Yu Garden & Old Town

Best in late afternoon

The traditional pavilions, rockeries, and red lanterns of Yu Garden create a timeless Chinese atmosphere. The zigzag bridge over the pond is a favorite composition for photographers.

🌳 French Concession Plane Trees

Best on a sunny morning

The tree-lined streets of the former French Concession, especially around Wukang Road and Fuxing Road, offer dappled light, art deco architecture, and a European-meets-Asian charm.

🌉 Waibaidu Bridge

Best at night

Shanghai's oldest steel bridge offers a perfect frame for the Pudong skyline. At night, the bridge lights and the illuminated towers create a postcard-perfect scene.

🎨 Tianzifang Alleyways

Best on a sunny afternoon

The narrow lanes of Tianzifang are filled with colorful murals, hanging laundry, traditional shikumen architecture, and quirky shop fronts. Every corner is a photo opportunity.

Food & Shopping in Shanghai

🍽️ Must-Try Shanghai Cuisine

Xiaolongbao (Soup Dumplings)

Shanghai's most famous dish. Delicate steamed dumplings filled with pork and hot broth. Din Tai Fung and Jia Jia Tang Bao serve the best.

Shengjianbao (Pan-Fried Buns)

Crispy-bottomed, juicy pork buns pan-fried in a huge cast-iron skillet. Yang's Fry-Dumpling is the city's most famous chain.

Hairy Crab (Da Zha Xie)

A seasonal delicacy from nearby Yangcheng Lake. Available October–December. The rich, sweet roe is considered the ultimate Shanghai luxury.

Shanghai-Style Braised Pork (Hong Shao Rou)

Tender pork belly braised in soy sauce, sugar, and spices until meltingly soft. A home-style favorite reflecting Shanghai's Jiangsu culinary heritage.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Nanjing Road (East & West)

The world's longest shopping street at 5.5 km. East Nanjing Road is neon-lit with international brands. West Nanjing Road features luxury flagships like Hermès, Gucci, and Tiffany.

Tianzifang

A maze of narrow alleyways in the French Concession filled with artisan shops, galleries, and cafés. Perfect for unique souvenirs and local art.

Plaza 66 & Jing'an

Shanghai's premier luxury shopping destination with flagship stores for Louis Vuitton, Chanel, and Prada. Connected to Jing'an Temple for a cultural contrast.

AP Plaza (Fake Market)

Located in the Science & Technology Museum metro station, this underground market is famous for souvenirs, silk, and electronics. Bargaining is expected and part of the fun.

🎁 What to Buy in Shanghai

Shanghai Silk

Shanghai has been a silk trading center for over 1,000 years. Look for soft silk scarves, qipao (cheongsam) dresses, and silk bedding. The best quality is found at the Shanghai Silk Museum or reputable shops on Nanjing Road. Your driver can recommend trustworthy vendors.

Shikumen Architecture Models

Miniature replicas of Shanghai's iconic lane houses make unique souvenirs. These intricate models capture the distinctive stone-gate architecture of the French Concession and Old Town. Available at Tianzifang and Xintiandi gift shops.

Chinese Tea & Teaware

Shanghai tea houses stock premium jasmine tea, Longjing green tea, and aged pu'er. Pair your tea with a Yixing clay teapot or porcelain gaiwan for an elegant gift set. The Tianshan Tea Market is the best place to buy in bulk.

Art Deco Prints & Posters

Shanghai's 1920s–30s golden era produced stunning Art Deco advertising posters. Reproductions of vintage travel posters, cigarette ads, and calendar girls make stylish, lightweight souvenirs. Best found at Tianzifang galleries and the Shanghai History Museum shop.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Shanghai

Meet the traditional arts that have defined Shanghai for centuries — and the artisans keeping them alive.

🎭 Shanghai Opera (Huju)

UNESCO-listed intangible heritage. Shanghai Opera (Huju) emerged in the early 20th century, blending local folk songs with modern theatrical forms. Known for its melodious singing and contemporary themes, it reflects Shanghai's unique position between tradition and modernity.

Where to experience it: Shanghai Huju Art Center, Tianchan Theatre, or the Shanghai Grand Theatre. Ask your driver to book tickets in advance.

🧵 Shanghai Embroidery (Gu Xiu)

One of China's four great embroidery styles, Gu Xiu is known for its fine needlework, realistic depictions of landscapes and figures, and subtle use of color. The technique requires years of training and produces works that are almost indistinguishable from paintings.

Where to find it: Shanghai Embroidery Institute in Xuhui District offers demonstrations and sales of museum-quality pieces. Tianzifang galleries also carry contemporary Gu Xiu works.

🎨 Shanghai School of Painting (Haipai)

A distinctive painting style that emerged in 19th-century Shanghai, blending traditional Chinese brush techniques with Western perspective and color. Haipai artists were known for their commercial savvy and innovative subjects, from birds and flowers to urban scenes.

Where to find it: Shanghai Chinese Painting Academy, Duoyunxuan Art Shop on Nanjing Road, and the Shanghai Museum's painting galleries.

🎋 Bamboo Carving (Jiading Bamboo)

Though originating in nearby Jiading District, this intricate art form is closely tied to Shanghai culture. Master carvers create miniature sculptures, calligraphy, and landscapes on bamboo roots and stems, using techniques passed down for over 400 years.

Where to find it: Jiading Bamboo Carving Museum, Shanghai Museum craft galleries, and select shops in the Old Town.

🎤 Storytelling (Shanghai Pingtan)

A traditional form of musical storytelling performed in the Wu dialect (Shanghainese). The performer alternates between singing and speaking, accompanied by a pipa or sanxian. Pingtan has been entertaining Shanghai audiences in teahouses for over 400 years.

Where to experience it: Shanghai Pingtan Troupe performances, or traditional teahouses in the Old Town. Ask your driver for current show schedules.

🪡 Qipao (Cheongsam) Tailoring

The iconic Chinese dress reached its peak of elegance in 1920s–30s Shanghai. Master tailors still hand-make qipao with intricate embroidery, frog buttons, and perfectly fitted silhouettes. A custom qipao is the ultimate Shanghai souvenir.

Where to find it: Changle Road in the French Concession has several traditional qipao tailors. Allow 3–5 days for a custom-made dress.

Shanghai Local Secrets

Insider tips to help you experience Shanghai like a local.

🌅

Beat the Bund Crowds at Dawn

The Bund is packed by 8 AM. Arrive at 5:30 AM to see tai chi practitioners, kite flyers, and the sunrise over Pudong. The light is golden and the crowds are nonexistent. Your driver can pick you up at your hotel before dawn.

🍜

Eat Breakfast Like a Shanghainese

Skip the hotel buffet and try cong you bing (scallion pancakes), you tiao (fried dough sticks), and dou jiang (soy milk) at a street stall. The best spots are on Wulumuqi Road and in the lanes behind Huaihai Road.

🚶

Explore the Hidden Lanes

Skip the touristy Nanluoguxiang equivalent and wander Julu Road, Changle Road, and Yongkang Road. These lanes have family-run cafés, vintage shops, and old Shanghainese courtyard houses without the crowds.

🌸

Seasonal Highlights

Spring: Cherry blossoms at Tongji University. Summer: Evening cruises on the Huangpu River. Autumn: Ginkgo trees at Shanghai Botanical Garden. Winter: Hot pot and cozy teahouses in the French Concession.

🚇

Avoid Rush Hour Metro

Shanghai's metro is packed from 7:30–9:30 AM and 5:30–7:30 PM. If you must travel during these hours, Line 2 and Line 1 are the worst. A private car is worth every yuan during rush hour.

🍽️

Book a Hairy Crab Season Trip

If visiting in October–December, don't miss hairy crab season. Take a day trip to Yangcheng Lake with your private driver, eat fresh crab at a lakeside restaurant, and bring a cooler box of live crabs back to your hotel.

Best Time to Visit Shanghai

Each season reveals a different face of the Pearl of the Orient.

🌸

Spring

Mar–May · 12–22°C

Mild and pleasant with occasional rain. Cherry blossoms at Tongji University. Comfortable for walking the Bund.

Highlights: French Concession walks, garden tours

☀️

Summer

Jun–Aug · 25–35°C

Hot, humid, and rainy with typhoon season. Air conditioning is essential. Evening Huangpu River cruises are magical.

Highlights: Night Bund views, indoor museums

🍂

Autumn

Sep–Nov · 15–25°C

The best season — clear skies, comfortable temperatures, and golden ginkgo trees. The most popular time to visit.

Highlights: Hairy crab season, outdoor dining

❄️

Winter

Dec–Feb · 2–10°C

Cold and damp with occasional light snow. Fewer tourists, lower hotel prices, and cozy teahouse culture.

Highlights: Hot pot, Chinese New Year celebrations
