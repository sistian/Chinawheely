From Village to Metropolis

Name Origin

The name "Shenzhen" (深圳) means "Deep Ditches," referring to the deep irrigation channels in the area's rice paddies. Before 1980, it was a small fishing village of 30,000 people just across the border from Hong Kong. In 1980, it became China's first Special Economic Zone (SEZ), opening to foreign investment and free-market reforms. Today, it's one of the world's wealthiest cities, home to tech giants like Huawei, Tencent, and DJI.

Education & Universities

Shenzhen has rapidly developed its educational infrastructure with world-class universities. Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech) is ranked as one of China's top young universities, and Shenzhen University is known for its strong engineering and design programs. The city also hosts campuses of international universities like Tsinghua Shenzhen and Peking University Shenzhen. The city's focus on innovation has created a unique educational ecosystem.

Metro & Transportation

Shenzhen's metro system has 17 lines covering 550+ km, making it one of the most efficient in China. The airport line connects Bao'an Airport (SZX) to Futian and Luohu districts in 40 minutes. The city also connects directly to Hong Kong via the Shenzhen Bay Port and Futian Checkpoint. However, with luggage, the metro can be challenging. Our private car service offers door-to-door convenience from the airport to any hotel or business district.

Natural Resources & Environment

Despite its rapid urbanization, Shenzhen has invested heavily in green spaces. The city has over 1,000 parks, including the massive Shenzhen Bay Park along the waterfront. The Wutong Mountains in the east offer hiking trails with views of Hong Kong. The city is located on the Pearl River Delta with a subtropical climate. Shenzhen's air quality is among the best in China's major cities due to strict environmental policies.

Getting to Shenzhen: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Shenzhen and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Shenzhen Bao'an International Airport (SZX)

West of the city · 35 km to Futian CBD

SZX is Shenzhen's main airport and one of southern China's busiest. It has a single large terminal with three concourses (A, B, C). The airport metro line connects to Futian and Luohu districts in 40 minutes, but with luggage a private car is far more comfortable. By road, expect 40–50 minutes to Futian or Nanshan districts.

Shenzhen Bao'an Airport (Satellite Terminal)

Expanding capacity · Same location

The airport is expanding with a satellite terminal to handle growing traffic. Current terminals handle flights to most major Asian cities, Europe, and Australia. Our drivers monitor terminal assignments and will meet you at the correct arrival hall.

🚄 Railway Stations

Shenzhen North Station

High-speed hub · 30 min to Futian CBD

The main station for high-speed trains to Guangzhou (30 min), Hong Kong (15 min to West Kowloon), Beijing (8 hours), and Wuhan (4 hours). It's one of China's busiest stations. A private car pickup is recommended as the station is large and taxi queues can be long.

Shenzhen Station (Luohu)

Border crossing · Direct to Hong Kong

Located at the Luohu border crossing, this station serves conventional trains to Guangzhou and connects directly to Hong Kong's MTR system. It's convenient for travelers heading to Hong Kong after their Shenzhen visit.

Futian Station

High-speed · CBD location

A modern underground station in the heart of Futian CBD, serving high-speed trains to Hong Kong (14 minutes to West Kowloon). It's extremely convenient for business travelers staying in the CBD.

Shenzhen West Station

Regional trains · Nanshan District

Mainly serves regional trains to western Guangdong and some freight services. Less frequently used by tourists but useful for certain regional routes.

Pingshan Station

Eastern Shenzhen · Pingshan District

Serves high-speed trains on the Xiamen–Shenzhen line. Convenient for travelers staying in eastern Shenzhen or visiting the Pingshan district.

Food & Shopping in Shenzhen

🍽️ Must-Try Shenzhen Cuisine

Dim Sum (Yum Cha)

Shenzhen's proximity to Hong Kong means excellent Cantonese dim sum. From shrimp dumplings (har gow) to BBQ pork buns (char siu bao), the quality rivals Hong Kong at a fraction of the price. Your driver can recommend the best dim sum restaurant in Luohu District.

Seafood at Dapeng Peninsula

Fresh seafood straight from the South China Sea. Dapeng Peninsula on the eastern coast is famous for its seafood restaurants. Choose your live fish, crab, or lobster from the tanks and have it cooked to order. Your driver can take you there for a memorable seafood feast.

Shenzhen-Style Hotpot

A lighter version of Sichuan hotpot, influenced by Cantonese cuisine. The broth is milder but still flavorful. Fresh seafood and thinly sliced beef are the highlights. Your driver knows the best hotpot spots in Nanshan District.

Luohu Street Food

The Luohu Commercial City area is a street food paradise, with stalls selling everything from grilled squid to mango pancakes. The energy is electric and the flavors are bold. Your driver can drop you at the best food street and pick you up after you've sampled everything.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Dongmen Pedestrian Street

Shenzhen's most famous shopping street with hundreds of stalls selling clothing, electronics, and accessories at bargain prices. Bargaining is expected and part of the fun. Your driver can navigate the crowded streets to drop you at the entrance.

Luohu Commercial City

A multi-story shopping complex famous for replica goods, custom tailoring, and electronics. Located right next to the Hong Kong border. Bargaining is essential. Your driver can help you negotiate and ensure you get a fair price.

COCO Park (Futian)

A modern open-air shopping mall in Futian District with international brands, trendy restaurants, and a vibrant nightlife scene. The surrounding area is Shenzhen's central business district. Your driver can pick you up after dinner.

Huaqiangbei Electronics Market

The world's largest electronics market, with dozens of multi-story buildings selling everything from smartphone parts to drones. Huaqiangbei is where many of the world's electronics are sourced. Your driver can navigate the maze-like market and help you find what you need.

🎁 What to Buy in Shenzhen

Electronics & Gadgets

Shenzhen is the world's electronics capital. Huaqiangbei Electronic Market offers everything from components to finished gadgets. You can find unique phone accessories, drones, smart home devices, and LED products at wholesale prices. Your driver can navigate the massive market with you.

Shenzhen Design & Creative Products

As a UNESCO City of Design, Shenzhen has a thriving creative product scene. Visit OCT Loft's design shops for innovative stationery, home decor, and tech accessories created by local designers. The Shenzhen Design Week (April) showcases the best of the city's creative output.

Pearls & Jewelry

The Shuibei Jewelry District is one of the world's largest jewelry trading centers. You can find high-quality pearls, gold, and gemstones at prices far below Western retail. Your driver can take you to reputable dealers and help with quality verification.

Shenzhen Tech Souvenirs

DJI drones, Huawei devices, and Xiaomi smart home products are all headquartered in Shenzhen. Buying directly from brand stores or authorized dealers can offer better prices than abroad. The DJI Flagship Store in MixC World is a must-visit for drone enthusiasts.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Shenzhen

Meet the traditional arts that have defined Shenzhen for centuries — and the artisans keeping them alive.

🎭 Cantonese Opera (Yueju)

UNESCO-listed intangible heritage. Though Shenzhen is a young city, Cantonese Opera remains an important cultural tradition brought by migrants from Guangdong. The elaborate costumes, face painting, and unique singing style continue to entertain audiences.

Where to experience it: Shenzhen Cantonese Opera Theatre or the Nanshan Cultural Center. Ask your driver to book tickets.

🎨 Dafen Oil Painting Village

Once the world's largest producer of oil paintings, Dafen Village in Shenzhen is home to thousands of artists who create reproductions of masterpieces and original works. The village has evolved from a copycat hub to a genuine art community with galleries and studios.

Where to find it: Dafen Oil Painting Village in Longgang District. Your driver can take you there for a fascinating art-buying experience.

🎋 Fish Lantern Dance (Shatoujiao)

A traditional folk dance from the Shatoujiao area of Shenzhen, performed during the Spring Festival. Dancers carry fish-shaped lanterns through the streets, symbolizing abundance and good fortune. The tradition reflects Shenzhen's fishing village origins before it became a metropolis.

Where to experience it: Shatoujiao during the Spring Festival, or at the Shenzhen Folk Culture Village.

🧵 Migrant Worker Poetry

A unique cultural phenomenon born from Shenzhen's factory culture. Migrant workers have formed poetry clubs and published anthologies that capture the dreams, struggles, and hopes of China's urbanization. The annual Migrant Worker Poetry Festival celebrates this grassroots literary movement.

Where to find it: Shenzhen Book City or the annual Migrant Worker Poetry Festival events.

🎤 Hakka Mountain Songs

The Hakka people, one of Shenzhen's original ethnic groups, have a tradition of improvised mountain songs that express love, labor, and daily life. These call-and-response songs are sung in the Hakka dialect and are recognized as an intangible cultural heritage.

Where to experience it: Hakka cultural villages in eastern Shenzhen, or during the Shenzhen Folk Culture Festival.

🎨 Shenzhen Design Week

As a UNESCO City of Design, Shenzhen hosts an annual Design Week that showcases innovative product design, architecture, and digital art. The city's design culture reflects its identity as a place where creativity and technology intersect.

Where to experience it: Various venues across the city during April. Check the official Design Week schedule.

Best Time to Visit Shenzhen

China's innovation capital enjoys a subtropical climate year-round.

🌸

Spring

Mar–May · 20–28°C

Warm and pleasant with occasional rain. Cherry blossoms at Shenzhen University. Perfect for outdoor activities and hiking.

Highlights: Wutong Mountain hiking, beach visits

☀️

Summer

Jun–Aug · 27–33°C

Hot, humid, and rainy with typhoon season. Beach days at Dameisha and indoor attractions like museums and malls are popular.

🍂

Autumn

Sep–Nov · 22–30°C

The best season — warm, dry, and sunny. Perfect for hiking, beach visits, and outdoor dining. The most comfortable time to explore.

Highlights: Hiking, coastal drives, outdoor festivals

Winter

Dec–Feb · 12–20°C

Mild and pleasant — Shenzhen's winter is like spring elsewhere. Perfect for exploring without the summer heat or crowds.

Highlights: Comfortable hiking, hot pot, mild beach days
