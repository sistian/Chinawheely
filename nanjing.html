Name Origin

The name "Nanjing" (南京) literally means "Southern Capital." It was the capital of six dynasties (Wu, Eastern Jin, Song, Qi, Liang, Chen) and later the Ming Dynasty before the capital moved to Beijing. During the Republic of China era, it served as the national capital again. The city's name reflects its status as a political and cultural center in southern China for over 1,700 years.

Education & Universities

Nanjing is one of China's top educational centers, home to Nanjing University (founded 1902, ranked top 5 nationally) and Southeast University (founded 1902, renowned for architecture and engineering). Other major institutions include Hohai University, Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics, and the prestigious Nanjing Arts Institute. The city hosts over 50 universities and research institutes.

Metro & Transportation

Nanjing's metro system has 13 lines and 500+ km of track, making it one of the most extensive in China. The airport line (S1) connects Lukou Airport to the city center in about 35 minutes. However, reaching the Purple Mountain scenic area, the Yangtze River bridges, and some historical sites requires transfers. A private car is the most convenient way to visit multiple sites like the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, and the Presidential Palace in one day.

Natural Resources & Environment

Nanjing straddles the Yangtze River, with the river flowing through the northern part of the city. Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan) dominates the eastern skyline, covered in forests and home to numerous historical sites. Xuanwu Lake, a vast imperial lake in the city center, offers scenic walks and boat rides. The city enjoys a subtropical climate with four distinct seasons and is famous for its plum blossoms in spring and colorful foliage in autumn.

Top Nanjing Attractions

From imperial mausoleums to Republican-era landmarks, explore Nanjing's rich heritage with a private driver.

🏯

Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

Purple Mountain • National Monument

The grand mausoleum of Dr. Sun Yat-sen, founder of modern China. Set on Purple Mountain with 392 steps leading to the memorial hall, it offers panoramic views over Nanjing. A private car takes you to the mountain base, saving energy for the climb.

🛕

Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

UNESCO World Heritage • Ming Dynasty

The tomb of the Hongwu Emperor, founder of the Ming Dynasty. The Sacred Way, lined with stone animals and officials, is one of China's most impressive imperial processional routes. The surrounding forest is stunning in autumn.

🏛️

Presidential Palace

Republican Era • Historical Complex

The former presidential office of the Republic of China, this vast complex blends Western and Chinese architecture. It covers the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom period and the Republican era. A private car is ideal for visiting this sprawling site.

🌸

Xuanwu Lake Park

Imperial Lake • City Center Oasis

A vast lake surrounded by walls, temples, and gardens. Once an imperial training ground, it's now Nanjing's favorite park. The five islands connected by bridges offer scenic walks, lotus viewing, and boat rides.

🗼

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

Qinhuai River • Night Market

A lively district centered around the ancient Confucius Temple, with the Qinhuai River running through it. The night market, lantern-lit boats, and traditional snack stalls create a magical atmosphere. Your driver can drop you off and pick you up after dinner.

🧱

Nanjing City Wall

Ming Dynasty • Longest City Wall

The longest, largest, and best-preserved city wall in the world, built during the Ming Dynasty. Walking along the wall offers views of the city, Xuanwu Lake, and the Purple Mountain. The Jiming Temple section is particularly scenic.

Getting to Nanjing: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Nanjing and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG)

South of the city · 35 km to city center

NKG is Nanjing's main airport, serving domestic and international flights across China and Asia. Located about 35 km south of the city center, it has a single modern terminal. The S1 Airport Metro Line connects to the city center, but with luggage after a long flight, a private car is far more comfortable and takes about 40–60 minutes to downtown hotels.

Nearby Airports

Hefei Xinqiao (HFE) · 170 km · Shanghai Pudong (PVG) · 280 km

Some travelers may arrive at Hefei Xinqiao (HFE) or Shanghai Pudong (PVG) and need ground transport to Nanjing. From Hefei, the drive takes about 2 hours; from Shanghai Pudong, about 3–3.5 hours. Our private car service can arrange long-distance transfers from either airport with an English-speaking driver.

🚄 Railway Stations

Nanjing South Station

Main high-speed hub · Yuhuatai District

The largest high-speed rail station in Nanjing, serving the Beijing-Shanghai and Shanghai-Wuhan-Chengdu lines. It connects Nanjing to Shanghai (1 hr), Beijing (3.5 hr), and Hangzhou (1.5 hr). A private car pickup is recommended as the station is vast and taxi queues can be long.

Nanjing Station

Historic central station · Xuanwu District

Located near Xuanwu Lake, this is Nanjing's oldest main station. It serves conventional trains to northern and eastern China, plus some high-speed services. Its central location makes hotel transfers quick and convenient.

Nanjing West Station

Freight & regional · Gulou District

Primarily used for freight and some regional passenger services. Located in the Gulou district, it is less relevant for most travelers but useful for certain regional routes.

Jiangning Station

Suburban HSR · Jiangning District

Serves the Jiangning district south of the city center with high-speed rail connections. Convenient for travelers visiting the Jiangning development zone or staying in the southern suburbs.

Xianlin Station

University district · Qixia District

Located near the Xianlin University Town, serving high-speed trains on the Shanghai-Nanjing intercity line. Convenient for travelers visiting the university district or staying in the eastern suburbs.

Nanjing's Best Photo Spots

Capture the ancient capital from the most iconic and photogenic angles.

🌅 Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum at Sunrise

Best at dawn

The grand staircase of the mausoleum, framed by ancient pines and the rising sun, creates a powerful image. The view from the top platform over the city is breathtaking on clear mornings.

🍂 Ming Xiaoling Sacred Way

Best in autumn

The stone statues of elephants, camels, and officials lining the Sacred Way are framed by golden ginkgo and red maple trees in autumn. This is one of Nanjing's most photographed spots.

🏮 Qinhuai River at Night

Best after sunset

The Confucius Temple area comes alive with red lanterns reflected in the Qinhuai River. Traditional boats drift past historic pavilions, creating a magical scene.

🌸 Xuanwu Lake and City Wall

Best in spring

The Ming City Wall curves along the edge of Xuanwu Lake, with cherry blossoms and willow trees in the foreground. The combination of ancient architecture and natural beauty is stunning.

🌿 Purple Mountain Forest Mist

Best in early morning mist

The misty forests of Purple Mountain create an ethereal atmosphere in the early morning. The interplay of light, mist, and ancient trees is a photographer's dream. Your driver can pick you up before dawn.

🎨 Nanjing Museum Courtyard

Best in soft afternoon light

The modern architecture of the Nanjing Museum, with its clean lines and reflecting pools, creates striking compositions. The contrast between the contemporary building and the ancient artifacts inside makes for compelling photography.

Food & Shopping in Nanjing

🍽️ Must-Try Nanjing Cuisine

Nanjing Salted Duck (Nanjing Banya)

Nanjing's most famous dish. Duck is brined in a mixture of salt, spices, and herbs, then air-dried. The result is tender, flavorful meat with a subtle saltiness. Hanguang and Shiziqiao restaurants serve the best.

Duck Blood Vermicelli Soup (Yaxue Fensi)

A beloved Nanjing street food. Duck blood, vermicelli noodles, and duck organs in a rich, savory broth. The best stalls are found near the Confucius Temple area.

Jinling Roast Duck

Nanjing's version of roast duck predates Beijing's famous Peking duck. The skin is crispy, the meat is succulent, and the flavor is slightly sweeter. A must-try for duck lovers.

Eight-Treasure Porridge (Laba Zhou)

A traditional sweet porridge made with glutinous rice, red beans, dates, and other ingredients. Originally from Nanjing's Qixia Temple, it's now enjoyed nationwide during the Laba Festival.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao) Area

Nanjing's most famous pedestrian shopping area, centered around the historic Confucius Temple. Features traditional shops, snack vendors, and souvenir stalls along the Qinhuai River. Best visited in the evening when the lanterns are lit.

Xinjiekou

Nanjing's premier commercial district with modern shopping malls, international brands, and department stores. The area around the Xinjiekou metro intersection is the city's busiest shopping hub.

Laomendong Historic District

A restored historic district with traditional architecture, artisan shops, and local snack vendors. Less touristy than the Confucius Temple area and more authentic. Your driver can recommend the best local snack shops.

Nanjing Cloud Brocade Museum

Learn about Nanjing's 1,600-year brocade tradition and buy authentic cloud brocade products. The museum shows the intricate weaving process and sells scarves, bags, and decorative items made with this UNESCO-listed craft.

🎁 What to Buy in Nanjing

Nanjing Cloud Brocade (Yunjin)

A UNESCO-listed intangible heritage, Nanjing cloud brocade is woven with gold and silk threads on traditional looms. Scarves, bags, and small decorative items make exquisite souvenirs. The Nanjing Cloud Brocade Museum is the best place to buy authentic pieces.

Nanjing Salted Duck

Vacuum-packed salted duck makes a practical and delicious souvenir. The best brands are Hanguang and Shiziqiao, available at the Confucius Temple area and major supermarkets. Ask your driver for recommendations on the freshest options.

Rain Flower Pebbles (Yuhua Shi)

Beautiful, colorful agate pebbles found in the Yangtze River near Nanjing. Legend says they were formed from the tears of a Buddhist monk. Small polished pebbles make unique, inexpensive souvenirs. Available at the Confucius Temple market.

Bamboo Carvings & Ink Stones

Nanjing has a long tradition of bamboo carving and ink stone craftsmanship. Small carved bamboo items and traditional ink stones make elegant gifts for calligraphy enthusiasts. Best found at Laomendong and the Confucius Temple craft shops.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Nanjing

Meet the traditional arts that have defined Nanjing for centuries — and the artisans keeping them alive.

🧵 Nanjing Cloud Brocade (Yunjin)

UNESCO-listed intangible heritage. Nanjing cloud brocade is one of the most exquisite silk weaving traditions in the world, with a history of over 1,600 years. The technique uses large, complex looms operated by two weavers — one above and one below — to create intricate patterns with gold and silk threads. The brocade was once reserved exclusively for imperial robes.

Where to experience it: Nanjing Cloud Brocade Museum on Chating Street offers demonstrations and sales of authentic pieces. Ask your driver to book a visit.

🎨 Jinling Woodblock Prints

A traditional printing technique that flourished during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Nanjing's woodblock prints are known for their delicate lines, rich colors, and themes drawn from history, literature, and daily life. The technique involves carving intricate designs into pear wood blocks and hand-printing each color layer.

Where to find it: Traditional print shops in the Laomendong district and the Nanjing Folk Museum.

🎭 Kunqu Opera in Nanjing

Nanjing has been a major center for Kunqu opera since the Ming Dynasty. The city's Kunqu troupes are known for their refined performances and preservation of classical repertoire. The Nanjing Kunqu Opera Theatre continues this tradition with regular performances.

Where to experience it: Nanjing Kunqu Opera Theatre, or the Jiangnan Gongyuan Theatre near the Confucius Temple. Ask your driver for current schedules.

🎋 Bamboo Carving (Jinling Zhu Diao)

Nanjing's bamboo carving tradition dates back to the Ming Dynasty. Master carvers create miniature sculptures, calligraphy, and landscapes on bamboo roots and stems. The style is known for its fine detail and elegant compositions, often depicting scenes from classical literature.

Where to find it: Craft shops in Laomendong and the Confucius Temple area. Some workshops offer demonstrations.

🎤 Nanjing Storytelling (Baiju)

A traditional form of storytelling performed in the Nanjing dialect. Baiju (白局) combines spoken narration with singing, accompanied by traditional instruments. The stories often depict local life, history, and legends of Nanjing.

Where to experience it: Traditional teahouses in the Confucius Temple area and cultural performances at the Nanjing Folk Museum.

🪡 Nanjing Kite Making

Nanjing has a long tradition of kite making, particularly the "sand swallow" kite style. The kites are hand-painted with intricate designs and flown during the Qingming Festival. The craft involves careful selection of bamboo, paper, and silk, and precise balancing for stable flight.

Where to find it: Traditional craft shops near the Confucius Temple and the Nanjing Folk Museum.

Nanjing Local Secrets

Insider tips to help you experience Nanjing like a local.

🌅

Beat the Crowds at Purple Mountain

Arrive at the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum at 7:00 AM when it opens. The morning light is magical, and you'll have the site almost to yourself before the tour buses arrive. Your driver can pick you up at your hotel early.

🍜

Eat Breakfast Like a Nanjingese

Skip the hotel buffet and try Salty Duck (Banya) with congee, Youtiao (fried dough), and Soy Milk at a street stall. The best spots are on Shiziqiao Street and in the lanes behind the Confucius Temple.

🚶

Explore the Hidden Alleys

Skip the crowded Confucius Temple and wander Laomendong instead. This restored historic district has fewer tourists, more local shops, and a more authentic atmosphere. Your driver can drop you at one end and pick you up at the other.

🌸

Seasonal Highlights

Spring: Cherry blossoms at Jiming Temple. Summer: Lotus flowers at Xuanwu Lake. Autumn: Golden ginkgo at Ming Xiaoling. Winter: Snow-covered city walls and cozy duck hot pot.

🚇

Avoid Rush Hour Metro

Nanjing's metro is packed from 7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM. If you must travel during these hours, Line 1 and Line 2 are the busiest. A private car is worth every yuan during rush hour.

🍽️

Book a Duck Feast

Nanjing is China's duck capital. Book a table at a renowned duck restaurant like Hanguang or Shiziqiao and try salted duck, roast duck, and duck blood soup in one meal. Your driver can make reservations.

Best Time to Visit Nanjing

Each season reveals a different face of the ancient southern capital.

🌸

Spring (Mar–May)

10–22°C · Mild & Blooming

Mild and pleasant with cherry blossoms at Jiming Temple and plum blossoms at Meihua Shan. Comfortable for walking the city walls and gardens.

☀️

Summer (Jun–Aug)

24–34°C · Hot & Humid

Hot and humid, but Xuanwu Lake boat rides and evening Qinhuai River walks are delightful. Afternoon thunderstorms are common. Book a car with AC for comfort.

🍂

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

15–24°C · Cool & Clear

The best time to visit. Golden ginkgo at Ming Xiaoling, red maples on Purple Mountain, and crisp clear skies. Perfect for photography and outdoor sightseeing.

❄️

Winter (Dec–Feb)

0–10°C · Cold & Damp

Cold and damp, but the city walls and museums are quiet. A dusting of snow transforms the ancient capital into a serene scene. Duck hot pot is the perfect winter meal.
