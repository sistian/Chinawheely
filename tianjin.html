Key Fact
About Tianjin

Name Origin

The name Tianjin (天津) means 'Emperor's Ford' — where the Yongle Emperor crossed the river. It has been a major port since the Yuan Dynasty and became a treaty port after the Second Opium War.

Education & Universities

Tianjin hosts Nankai University and Tianjin University, two of China's oldest and most prestigious universities, plus Tianjin Medical University.

Metro & Transportation

Tianjin Metro has 10 lines covering the city and connecting to Beijing via intercity rail. A private car is convenient for visiting the spread-out historic concession area and riverfront.

Natural Resources & Environment

Tianjin sits at the confluence of the Haihe River and the Bohai Sea. The flat city has hot summers and cold winters, with the riverfront and former concessions defining the urban landscape.

Getting to Tianjin: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Tianjin and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Tianjin Binhai International Airport (TSN)

East of city center · 15 km

A major airport serving northern China with domestic and international flights. A private car takes 30–45 minutes to downtown.

Nearby Airports

Beijing Capital (PEK), Beijing Daxing (PKX)

Many travelers use Beijing's airports and take the high-speed train or private car to Tianjin. The Beijing-Tianjin intercity train takes 30 minutes.

🚄 Railway Stations

Tianjin Railway Station

City center · Haihe Riverfront

Historic main station serving high-speed trains to Beijing (30 min), Shanghai, and other cities.

Tianjin South Railway Station

Suburban south · Main HSR

Major HSR station serving long-distance trains to Shanghai, Qingdao, and Jinan.

Tianjin West Railway Station

Western district · HSR hub

Major HSR station serving trains to Beijing, Shanghai, Taiyuan, and other destinations.

Tianjin North Railway Station

Old station · Limited service

Historic station with limited passenger service, mainly regional trains.

Binhai Railway Station

Tanggu district · Near port

Serves the Binhai New Area and coastal district.

Food & Shopping in Tianjin

🍽️ Must-Try Tianjin Cuisine

Goubuli Baozi

Famous steamed pork buns with 18 pleats. A Tianjin icon, though locals often prefer smaller shops.

Jianbing Guozi

Crispy breakfast crepe with egg, fried dough, scallions, and sauce. Originated in Tianjin.

Erduoyan Fried Rice Cake

Sweet rice cakes fried crispy outside and chewy inside. Classic Tianjin snack.

Mahua

Crispy twisted fried dough sticks with sesame. Popular snack and souvenir.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Tianjin

Meet the traditional arts that have shaped Tianjin's identity for centuries.

🎭 Tianjin Crosstalk

Traditional comedic dialogue famous for quick wit and Tianjin dialect.

Where to find it: Mingliu Tea House and theaters on Ancient Culture Street.

🎨 Yangliuqing Woodblock Prints

Over 400 years of hand-printed New Year paintings.

Where to find it: Yangliuqing town and Ancient Culture Street shops.

🧵 Nirenzhang Clay Sculpture

Hand-molded clay figurines dating back to the Qing Dynasty.

Where to find it: Ancient Culture Street and Tianjin Folk Museum.

🎵 Jingyun Dagu Storytelling

Stories sung to drum accompaniment, a northern Chinese tradition.

Where to find it: Traditional theaters in the old city.

🪁 Tianjin Kite Making

Elaborate bamboo-frame kites with painted designs.

Where to find it: Spring kite festivals and craft shops.

🏮 Tianjin Paper Cutting

Intricate paper cuttings for festivals and decoration.

Where to find it: Folk art markets and cultural shops.

Tianjin Local Secrets

Insider tips to help you experience Tianjin like a local.

🎭

Catch a Crosstalk Show

Tianjin crosstalk is best experienced in a teahouse. Even if you don't understand Chinese, the atmosphere is lively.

🍳

Eat Jianbing for Breakfast

Tianjin-style jianbing is the original. Look for street vendors in the morning.

🚗

Use a Car for the Concessions

Five Great Avenues is large and spread out. A car lets you cover more ground in one day.

🌃

See the Tianjin Eye at Night

The Ferris wheel is best after dark. The riverside walk is romantic and lively.

🍺

Skip the Famous Goubuli Shop

Locals say the famous Goubuli restaurant is overpriced. Try smaller neighborhood baozi shops for better value.

🚄

Day Trip to Beijing

The high-speed train to Beijing takes 30 minutes. A private car takes 2 hours but is more flexible.

Best Time to Visit Tianjin

Plan your trip around Tianjin's seasons for the best experience.

🌸

Spring (Apr–May)

12–24°C · Mild

Pleasant temperatures and blooming flowers. Ideal for walking the concessions.

☀️

Summer (Jun–Aug)

25–35°C · Hot

Hot and humid. Evening river walks are pleasant. Air conditioning is essential.

🍂

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

15–25°C · Cool

The best season for golden ginkgo trees and comfortable sightseeing.

❄️

Winter (Dec–Mar)

-5–5°C · Cold

Cold and windy, especially near the river. Indoor museums and hot food are highlights.

Useful Chinese Phrases for Tianjin

A few key phrases to help you navigate Tianjin.

你好 (Nǐ hǎo)

Hello

Basic greeting.

谢谢 (Xièxiè)

Thank you

Use after receiving help.

我要一碗狗不理包子 (Wǒ yào yì wǎn Gǒubùlǐ bāozi)

I want a bowl of Goubuli buns

Food phrase for Tianjin's famous dish.

这个多少钱？ (Zhège duōshao qián?)

How much is this?

Useful for shopping.

我要去五大道 (Wǒ yào qù Wǔdàdào)

I want to go to Five Great Avenues

Tell your driver your destination.

厕所在哪里？ (Cèsuǒ zài nǎlǐ?)

Where is the bathroom?

Essential at tourist sites.
