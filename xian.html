Name Origin

The name "Xi'an" (西安) means "Western Peace." The city was known as Chang'an (长安, "Eternal Peace") for most of its history and served as the capital for 13 dynasties including the Zhou, Qin, Han, Sui, and Tang. As the eastern terminus of the Silk Road, Chang'an was the world's largest city 1,300 years ago with over a million residents. The name was changed to Xi'an in 1369 during the Ming Dynasty.

Education & Universities

Xi'an is one of China's most important educational centers with over 60 universities. Xi'an Jiaotong University (founded 1896, one of China's top 10) and Northwestern Polytechnical University (China's premier aerospace and aviation school) are the most prestigious. The city produces more engineers and scientists than almost anywhere else in China, earning it the nickname "China's Silicon Valley of the West."

Metro & Transportation

Xi'an's metro system has 9 lines covering 300+ km. The airport line connects Xianyang International Airport (XIY) to the North Railway Station in 45 minutes. However, the metro doesn't reach many major tourist sites like the Terracotta Warriors. Our private car service is the most convenient way to visit the Warriors and other suburban attractions. The city is also a major rail hub with high-speed connections to Beijing (4.5 hours) and Shanghai (6 hours).

Natural Resources & Environment

Xi'an sits in the fertile Guanzhong Plain, surrounded by the Qinling Mountains to the south and the Loess Plateau to the north. The Wei River flows through the city, and the nearby Black River supplies much of the city's water. The Qinling Mountains are a biodiversity hotspot, home to the giant panda and golden monkey. Xi'an experiences a semi-humid continental climate with hot summers and cold winters.

Top Xi'an Attractions

Discover the treasures of China's ancient capital with a private driver.

🗿

Terracotta Warriors

UNESCO World Heritage • Qin Dynasty

One of the world's greatest archaeological discoveries — 8,000 life-sized terracotta soldiers guarding the tomb of Emperor Qin Shi Huang. Located 40 km east of the city. Our private car service includes the 1-hour drive with a knowledgeable driver who knows the best viewing times.

🏰

Xi'an City Wall

Ming Dynasty • 14 km Fortification

The most complete city wall in China, built during the Ming Dynasty. 14 km around the old city, 12 meters high and 15 meters wide. You can cycle the entire wall or enjoy the views from the South Gate. Our driver can drop you at any gate and pick you up at another.

🕌

Muslim Quarter & Great Mosque

Tang Dynasty • Islamic Heritage

A vibrant maze of narrow streets behind the Drum Tower, filled with street food vendors, souvenir shops, and the Great Mosque of Xi'an (founded 742 AD, one of China's oldest). The night market is a must-visit for food lovers. Your driver can drop you at the main entrance and arrange a pickup after dinner.

🗼

Giant Wild Goose Pagoda

Tang Dynasty • Buddhist Landmark

Built in 652 AD during the Tang Dynasty to house Buddhist scriptures brought from India by the monk Xuanzang. The surrounding Da Ci'en Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The nearby fountain plaza hosts China's largest musical fountain show.

🏛️

Shaanxi History Museum

National Museum • Ancient Artifacts

One of China's finest museums, housing over 370,000 artifacts spanning from prehistoric times to the Qing Dynasty. The Tang Dynasty gold and silver collection is world-renowned. Admission is free but queues can be long — your driver can help you navigate the ticketing system.

🌄

Mount Huashan

120 km East • Sacred Mountain

One of China's Five Great Mountains, famous for its sheer cliff faces and perilous hiking trails. A 2-hour drive from Xi'an. For a more comfortable experience, your driver can take you to the cable car entrance and wait while you explore the summit. Return to Xi'an in the evening.

Getting to Xi'an: Airports & Railway Stations

Everything you need to know about arriving in Xi'an and reaching your hotel.

✈️ Airports

Xi'an Xianyang International Airport (XIY)

Northwest of the city · 40 km to Bell Tower

XIY is Xi'an's main airport and one of northwest China's busiest gateways. It has three terminals: Terminal 1 (regional flights), Terminal 2 (domestic flights), and Terminal 3 (most international and major domestic flights). The airport metro line connects to the North Railway Station in 45 minutes, but with luggage after a long flight, a private car is the most comfortable option. By road, expect 50–60 minutes to downtown hotels near the Bell Tower.

Xi'an Xianyang Airport (New Terminal 5 — Planned)

Expanding capacity · Same location

Xi'an is rapidly expanding its airport capacity to serve growing international traffic. The existing terminals handle flights to Seoul, Tokyo, Bangkok, and major European cities. Our drivers monitor terminal assignments and will meet you at the correct arrival hall.

🚄 Railway Stations

Xi'an North Station

High-speed hub · 30 min to Bell Tower

The main station for high-speed trains to Beijing (4.5 hours), Shanghai (6 hours), Zhengzhou, and Luoyang. The station is modern and massive — a pre-booked pickup with a clear meeting point saves significant time.

Xi'an Station

Historic central station · 10 min to Bell Tower

Located near the city wall, this is Xi'an's traditional main station. It serves conventional trains to western and northern China, including routes to Lanzhou, Urumqi, and Tibet. Its central location makes hotel transfers quick.

Xi'an South Station

Regional trains · Southern suburbs

Mainly serves regional trains to nearby cities and mountainous areas in southern Shaanxi. Less frequently used by tourists but useful for trips to the Qinling Mountains.

Xi'an East Station (Freight)

Freight hub · East of the city

Primarily a freight station, though some regional passenger services operate here. Not commonly used by tourists.

Huashan North Station

For Mount Huashan · 120 km east

If you're taking the high-speed train to Mount Huashan, this is your station. From there, our driver can pick you up and take you to the mountain entrance, or you can book a direct private car from Xi'an.

Xi'an's Best Photo Spots

Capture the city from the most iconic and photogenic angles.

🗿 Terracotta Warriors at Opening

Best at 8:30 AM

Arrive when the gates open to capture the warriors in Pit 1 without the crowds. The morning light filtering through the hall creates a dramatic atmosphere. Your driver can get you there from downtown in under an hour.

🏰 Xi'an City Wall at Sunset

Best at golden hour

Cycle or walk the South Gate section at sunset for stunning views of the old city rooftops, Bell Tower, and modern skyline beyond. The warm light on the ancient brickwork is magical.

🕌 Muslim Quarter Night Market

Best after dark

The neon signs, red lanterns, and steam from street food stalls create a vibrant, atmospheric scene. The narrow alleyways around the Great Mosque are especially photogenic in the evening.

🌄 Giant Wild Goose Pagoda Fountain

Best at evening show

The musical fountain show in front of the pagoda is one of China's largest. The illuminated pagoda reflected in the water, with colored fountains dancing in sync, makes for spectacular night photography.

🌸 Qinglong Temple Cherry Blossoms

Best in late March

This ancient temple is one of Xi'an's best-kept secrets for cherry blossom viewing. The pink blooms frame the traditional architecture beautifully, with far fewer crowds than Wuhan University.

🏔️ Mount Huashan Plank Walk

Best at sunrise

For the adventurous photographer, the perilous plank walk on Mount Huashan's south peak offers heart-stopping views and dramatic cliff-face shots. Not for the faint of heart.

Food & Shopping in Xi'an

🍽️ Must-Try Xi'an Cuisine

Roujiamo (Chinese Hamburger)

Shredded braised pork stuffed in a crispy flatbread. Xi'an's most famous street food. Look for the "Zhouzhi" style with extra spices. Your driver can recommend the best roujiamo stall near the Muslim Quarter.

Yangrou Paomo (Mutton Soup)

A hearty lamb stew with hand-torn flatbread soaked in rich broth. A beloved Xi'an tradition. The best restaurants are in the Muslim Quarter. A warming meal, especially during Xi'an's cold winters.

Liangpi (Cold Skin Noodles)

Chewy wheat noodles tossed in chili oil, vinegar, and garlic. A refreshing street snack perfect for summer. Found at every street corner in Xi'an. Your driver knows the vendors with the best chili oil.

Biangbiang Noodles

Wide, hand-pulled noodles as thick as a belt, served with chili oil, vinegar, and vegetables. Named after the sound of the noodles slapping against the counter. The Chinese character has 58 strokes — one of the most complex in the language.

🛍️ Shopping Destinations

Muslim Quarter Night Market

The best place for souvenirs, from Terracotta Warrior replicas to traditional calligraphy. Bargain hard. The night market is also a food paradise. Your driver can drop you off and arrange a safe pickup after dinner.

Tang West Market

A modern recreation of the ancient Silk Road market, with shops selling jade, silk, tea, and antiques. The museum here has artifacts from the original Tang Dynasty market. Great for high-quality souvenirs.

Shu Yuan Men (Calligraphy Street)

A pedestrian street near the South Gate filled with shops selling calligraphy brushes, ink, paper, and traditional art supplies. A great place to pick up unique gifts for art lovers. Your driver can drop you at the entrance.

Century Ginwa Shopping Mall

Xi'an's most modern shopping center, with international brands, a cinema, and restaurants. Located near the High-Tech Zone. Your driver can pick you up after shopping and take you directly to the airport or your hotel.

🎁 What to Buy in Xi'an

Terracotta Warrior Replicas

High-quality replicas of the famous warriors make iconic souvenirs. Look for hand-painted ceramic versions from reputable shops near the museum or on Shu Yuan Men. Avoid the cheap plastic ones sold by street vendors.

Tang Dynasty Tri-Color Glazed Ceramics (Sancai)

A signature art form of the Tang Dynasty, these glazed pottery pieces feature rich amber, green, and cream colors. Reproductions of horses, camels, and figurines are popular. The Tang West Market has the best selection.

Shaanxi Paper-Cuts & Folk Art

Northern Shaanxi paper-cutting is bold and rustic, featuring animals, folk tales, and auspicious symbols. These red cuttings are lightweight, meaningful gifts. Find them at the folk art shops on Shu Yuan Men.

Chinese Calligraphy Supplies

Xi'an is a center for traditional calligraphy. Buy brushes, ink sticks, rice paper, and ink stones from the shops on Shu Yuan Men. Your driver can help you find authentic Four Treasures of the Study sets.

Intangible Cultural Heritage & Living Crafts of Xi'an

Meet the traditional arts that have defined Xi'an for centuries — and the artisans keeping them alive.

🎭 Qinqiang Opera

UNESCO-listed intangible heritage. The oldest and most influential of China's "Four Great Characteristic Melodies," Qinqiang is known for its bold, loud singing style and dramatic face-changing techniques. It originated in Shaanxi over 2,000 years ago.

Where to experience it: Yi Su She Theatre or the Shaanxi Grand Theatre. Ask your driver to book tickets.

🪕 Shaanxi Folk Music (Laosheng)

Traditional Shaanxi folk songs are characterized by their high-pitched, resonant vocals that echo across the Loess Plateau. The "Xintianyou" style is a call-and-response form that has been sung by farmers for centuries.

Where to experience it: Folk music performances at the Shaanxi Folk Art Museum or during the Spring Festival temple fair.

🖌️ Shaanxi Shadow Puppetry (Piying)

A traditional art form where leather puppets are manipulated behind a translucent screen, accompanied by music and storytelling. Shaanxi shadow puppets are carved from donkey leather and painted with vivid colors.

Where to find it: Shadow puppet workshops on the Muslim Quarter, or watch a performance at the Shaanxi Folk Art Museum.

🏺 Yaozhou Celadon

One of China's oldest and most celebrated ceramic traditions, originating near Xi'an during the Tang Dynasty. Yaozhou celadon is known for its jade-green glaze and intricate carved or incised patterns of flowers, fish, and clouds.

Where to find it: Yaozhou Kiln Museum (near Tongchuan, 1.5 hours from Xi'an) or reputable ceramic shops in the city center.

🧵 Shaanxi Embroidery (Xiu)

Traditional Shaanxi embroidery features bold colors and folk motifs including tigers, fish, and flowers. The style is more rustic and expressive than the refined Suzhou embroidery, reflecting the region's frontier heritage.

Where to find it: Folk art shops on Shu Yuan Men and the Shaanxi Folk Art Museum.

🎨 Tang Dynasty Music & Dance

Revived performances of Tang Dynasty court music and dance, featuring ancient instruments like the pipa, guzheng, and bamboo flute. The elegant choreography and flowing silk costumes transport audiences back to Chang'an's golden age.

Where to experience it: Tang Dynasty Palace Theatre near the Giant Wild Goose Pagoda offers nightly performances.

Xi'an Local Secrets

Insider tips to help you experience Xi'an like a local.

🗿

Visit the Warriors at 8:30 AM Sharp

The Terracotta Warriors open at 8:30 AM. Arrive at the gate 15 minutes early to be first in line. The first hour is blissfully quiet — by 10 AM, tour buses arrive en masse. Your driver can get you there right on time.

🍜

Eat Roujiamo from the Source

The best roujiamo is found in the Muslim Quarter's back alleys, not the main tourist street. Look for a stall with a queue of locals. The "Zhouzhi" style with extra spices is the local favorite.

🚲

Cycle the City Wall at Dusk

Rent a bike on the South Gate and cycle the full 14 km of the wall. The views at sunset are unbeatable, and the temperature is cooler. The rental shops close at 8 PM in summer, 6 PM in winter.

🌸

Seasonal Highlights

Spring: Cherry blossoms at Qinglong Temple. Summer: Cool evenings on the city wall. Autumn: Golden ginkgo on Yanta Road. Winter: Hot lamb soup in the Muslim Quarter.

🏛️

Book the Shaanxi History Museum Online

Free tickets are limited and queues are long. Book online at least a week ahead. The museum is closed on Mondays. A Tuesday or Wednesday morning visit is usually the quietest.

🚗

Use a Private Car for Huashan

Public buses to Mount Huashan take 2+ hours and are crowded. A private car gets you there in under 2 hours, and your driver can wait while you hike, then take you to a local restaurant for a well-earned meal.
